VAE derivation for Gaussian case

In Appendix B of the VAE paper by Kingma and Welling, they derive the KL divergence for the scenario in which $q(\textbf{z})$ and $p(\textbf{z})$ are both Gaussian. I do not understand this step: $$ \begin{align} \int q_{\theta}(\textbf{z})\log p(\textbf{z}) d\textbf{z} &= \int \mathcal{N}(\textbf{z}; \boldsymbol{\mu}, \boldsymbol{\sigma}^2) \log \mathcal{N}(\textbf{z}; \textbf{0}, \textbf{1}) \\ &= - \frac{J}{2} \log(2 \pi) - \frac{1}{2} \sum_{j=1}^{J} (\mu_j^2 + \sigma_j^2) \end{align} $$ The first equality is fine. Can someone explain how to get from line 1 to 2?
