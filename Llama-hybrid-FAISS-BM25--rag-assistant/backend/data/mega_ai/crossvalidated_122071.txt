You don't need to specify a covariance structure and it is highly discouraged: If you choose the wrong structure, you might miss the targeted type-I-error. Instead, use the procedure described here . It is a generalization of ANOVA for unknown covariance matrices and even applicable if there are more repeated measures than independent subjects. Unfortunately, it is not (yet) implemented in SPSS. But there are SAS macros . See how hld-f2.sas is used.
