Why can MA(q) (Moving Average Model with lag q) not predict q timesteps in the future

I understand that the equation for Moving Average model for lag q is - $$ y_t = \mu + \epsilon_t + \theta_1\epsilon_{t-1}+ \theta_2\epsilon_{t-2}+\dots+ \theta_q\epsilon_{t-q} $$ lets say we have training data of 100 timesteps and test data of 10 timesteps. and lag q = 2 I first evaluate the coefficients $\theta_1,\theta_2,\dots,\mu$ assuming $\epsilon_0=0\epsilon_{-1}=0$ , then I evaluate $\epsilon_1, \epsilon_2,\dots,\epsilon_{100}$ using the above equation $$\epsilon_1 = y_1-\mu-\theta_1\epsilon_0-\theta_2\epsilon_{-1}=y_1-\mu=f(y_1)$$ $$\epsilon_2 = y_2-\mu-\theta_1\epsilon_1-\theta_2\epsilon_{0}=y_1-\mu=f(y_2, y_1)$$ $$\vdots$$ so on and so forth to calculate $\epsilon_{100}$ since it will be $f(y_{100},y_{99},\dots,y_1)$ This means I could evaluate $y_{101}$ , then $\epsilon_{101}$ using predicted $y_{101}$ , then go on to $y_{102}, \epsilon_{102}$ and continue till $y_{110}$ . This means that we can predict any number of values in the future and not be restricted to just $2$ values in the future i.e., lag. Please help me understand where I am going wrong.
