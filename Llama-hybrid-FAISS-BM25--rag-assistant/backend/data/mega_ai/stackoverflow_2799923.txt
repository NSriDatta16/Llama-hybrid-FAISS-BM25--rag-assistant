Notice that the SLA lists the maximum (worst case)... the actual performance you see is likely to be better. Their are a number of ways to tune the performance of your storage access. The most important thing is to colocate your compute and your storage. That means you should put them in the same affinity group when you create your application and storage account. If you ask this question over on the Windows Azure MSDN forum , you might get more detailed responses.
