Distribution of Poisson variable choose 2

I have a random Poisson variable, $$X\sim Po(\lambda)$$ Also, the value of $X$ determines the value of $Y$: $$X=k\Rightarrow Y={k\choose{2}}= \frac{k^{2}-k}{2}$$ I guess there is no elegant way of writing a PMF, other than: $$\text{Pr}\left[Y={k\choose{2}}\right]=\text{Pr}\left[X=k\right]$$ What more can we say about the distribution of $Y$? I guess the moments are easy to deduce? Thanks in advance
