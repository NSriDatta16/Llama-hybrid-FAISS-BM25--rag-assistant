You have stumbled upon the famous koan: Closures are a poor man's object. You are using Action to substitute for a property getter of type T . Such a thing would be (slightly) less of a dirty trick in a more dynamic language, since it could be implemented by injecting a getter that’s decorated with your logging function, but in C# there isn’t an elegant way to monkeypatch someone’s property when they’re not expecting it.
