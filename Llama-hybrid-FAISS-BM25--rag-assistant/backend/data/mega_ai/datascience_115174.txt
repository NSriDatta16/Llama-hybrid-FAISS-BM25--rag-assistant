Memory-augmented Neural Networks (MANNs) sound kind-of similar to what you are describing. In this architecture, the network acts as a controller that can read and write from a block of external long-term memory. MANNs are still in the early stages of research and development; last I heard, they were very difficult to train but show some promising results when successfully trained. Here's a paper that applies MANNs to some NLP problems: https://aclanthology.org/D17-3005/
