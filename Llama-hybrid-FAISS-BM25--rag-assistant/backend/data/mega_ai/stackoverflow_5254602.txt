Whether this is a good or bad idea depends on your goals. You're right that UserAgent strings are easily spoofed so it's not at all reliable but does that really matter for your trials? Is inadvertently blocking a few users (those with wrong or unrecognized UA strings) really relevant when, by design, your blocking out the vast majority of users (anyone using a desktop browser)?
