Android does a lot of work on its own in order to get stuff to look well on almost all screens. Taken from: Supporting multiple screens At run time, the platform provides three types of support to your application, to ensure the best possible display on the current device screen: 1) Pre-scaling of resources (such as image assets) 2) Auto-scaling of pixel dimensions and coordinates 3) Compatibility-mode display on larger screen-sizes Specifically, since that app only has hdpi images, it will downsize them to look well on a mdpi and ldpi screen.
