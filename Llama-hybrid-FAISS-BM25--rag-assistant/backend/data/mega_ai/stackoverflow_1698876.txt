Here are some examples: The in-memory representation of a parsed program or expression is a tree. In the case of expressions (excluding ternary operators) the tree will be binary. The components of a GUI are organized as a tree. Any "containment" hierarchy can be represented as a tree. (HTML, XML and SGML are examples. And of course, binary (and n-ary) trees can be used to represent indexes, maps, sets and other "generic" data structures.
