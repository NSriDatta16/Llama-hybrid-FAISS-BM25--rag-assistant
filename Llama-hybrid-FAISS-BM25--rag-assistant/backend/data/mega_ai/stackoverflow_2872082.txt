I would always encourage you to use Method 2. It makes life less complicated. The term for that kind of primary key is a Surrogate Key, as opposed to a natural key which is based on other fields which have another purpose other than being a primary key. You can still enforce a unique constraint on the 3 foreign key fields at the database level without having to use them in the primary key. Have a read of this article http://www.wingenious.com/article1.html and then if you are interested in this, here is a series of articles on the same subject: http://www.wingenious.com/articles.html
