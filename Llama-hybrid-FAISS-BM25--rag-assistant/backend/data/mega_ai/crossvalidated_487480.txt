How to calculate Markov Chain parameters knowing AR(1) coefficient $\rho$?

Given that a discrete-time two-states (low and high) Markov chain $X_t$ can take two values: \begin{pmatrix} X_l\\ X_h \end{pmatrix} with its transition probability matrix $P^{T}$ : \begin{pmatrix} p_1 & 1-p_1 \\ 1-p_2 & p_2 \end{pmatrix} If the mean, standard deviation, and first-order autocorrelation of $X_t$ are $\mu$ , $\sigma$ , and $\rho$ respectively. How can I calculate the values of $X_l$ , $X_h$ , $p_1$ , and $p_2$ . Are three moments insufficient for four unknowns? Following up: If I introduce the long-term (or called 'initial') state probabilities: $\pi = ({\pi}_1,{\pi}_2)^T$ , then I will meet a similar situation to solve 6 unknowns with 5 equations: \begin{equation} \pi_1X_l + \pi_2X_h = \mu \end{equation} \begin{equation} \pi_1(X_l-\mu)^2 + \pi_2(X_h-\mu)^2 = \sigma^2 \end{equation} \begin{equation} \pi_1p_1X_l^2+\pi_1(1-p_1)X_lX_h + \pi_2p_2X_h^2 + \pi_2(1-p_2)X_hX_l = E[X_tX_{t-1}]=\rho E[X_{t-1}^2] = \rho(\mu^2+\sigma^2) \end{equation} \begin{equation} \pi_1 + \pi_2 = 1 \end{equation} \begin{equation} P\pi = \pi \end{equation}
