One feasible approach is moment-matching : finding parameters to match the first four moments of the data. In this case $X=L+N$ with independent lognormal $L$ and normal $N$ normal, so (for example) \begin{align} E[X^2]&=E[L^2]\phantom{\mu_1+2\sigma_1^2)}+2\,E[L]\,E[N]\phantom{\mu_1\sigma_1^2/2)}+E[N^2]\\ &=\exp(2\mu_1+2\sigma_1^2)+2\mu_2\exp(\mu_1+\sigma_1^2/2) + (\mu_2^2+\sigma_2^2) \end{align} which uses standard formulas for the moments of the lognormal and normal summands. This gives an equation in $\mu_1,\sigma_1,\mu_2,\sigma_2$ , saying that the above expression should equal the average of the squares of the datapoints. This and similar equations for $E[X], E[X^3], E[X^4]$ can then be solved numerically for $\mu_1, \sigma_1, \mu_2, \sigma_2$ .
