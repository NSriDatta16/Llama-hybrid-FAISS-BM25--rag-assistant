Both your solutions run stratified sampling, meaning you remove the variance due to the random choice of state. Your resulting estimator is a weighted average of both estimators, with variance $0.2^2 \frac{P_1(1-P_1)}{500}+0.8^2\frac{P_2(1-P_2)}{500}=0.8\,10^{-4}\,P_1(1-P_1)+1.28\,10^{-3}\,P_2(1-P_2)$ $0.5^2\left[\frac{P_1(1-P_1)}{200}+\frac{P_2(1-P_2)}{800}\right]=1.25\,10^{-3}\,P_1(1-P_1)+3.125\,10^ {-4}\,P_2(1-P_2)$ Hence it all depends on the relative values of $P_1$ and $P_2$. By comparison, your original estimator can be written as $$10^{-3}[\hat{N}\hat{P_1}+(10^3-\hat{N})\hat{P_2}]$$ with $\hat{N}$ a Binomial Bin$(10^3,.2)$, $\hat{N}\hat{P_1}\sim\text{Bin}(\hat{N},P_1)$ and $(10^3-\hat{N})\hat{P_2}\sim\text{Bin}(10^3-\hat{N},P_2)$. Its variance is equal to $$10^{-6}\left[.2\times.8\,10^3\,[P_1+P_2]+\mathbb{E}\left\{\hat{N}^2\frac{P_1(1-P_1)}{\hat{N}}+(10^3-\hat{N})^2\frac{P_2(1-P_2)}{(10^3-\hat{N})}\right\}\right]$$by the decomposition $$\text{var}(\delta)=\text{var}\mathbb{E}[\delta|\hat{N}]+\mathbb{E}[\text{var}(\delta|\hat{N})]$$that is $$.16\,10^{-3}[P_1+P_2]+.2\,10^{-3}\,P_1(1-P_1)+.8\,10^{-3}\,P_2(1-P_2)$$
