jQuery to the rescue! $(document).ready(function() { $('#myTextArea').mouseleave( function(){ $('#myHiddenP').html($(this).val()); }); }); Thinking about how you use a textarea, you click on it, you type, and then you move the mouse to do something else. This event will only fire when the mouse moves outside of the textbox area (like to go click file->print). When ever this happens, we simply stash the contents of the textbox in a hidden paragraph. The html: Then in your screen stylesheet: #myTextArea{ height: 1400px; display: block;} #myHiddenP { display: none;} and your print stylesheet: #myTextArea {display: none;} #myHiddenP {display: block; height: 1400px;}
