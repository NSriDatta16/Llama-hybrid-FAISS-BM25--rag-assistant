From Statistics Canada, Seasonal adjustment and trend-cycle estimation : Identifiable seasonality is defined as a seasonal pattern that is not obscured by a high degree of irregular fluctuations and thus can be identified reliably (Lothian and Morry, 1978). The paper cited for this definition is: Lothian, J. and Morry, M. (1978). A test for the presence of identifiable seasonality when using the X-11 program. Research paper 78-10-002E, Seasonal Adjustment and Time Series Staff, Statistics Canada.
