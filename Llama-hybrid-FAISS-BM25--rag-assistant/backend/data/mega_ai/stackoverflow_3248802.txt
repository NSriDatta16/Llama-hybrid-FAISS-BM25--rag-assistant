By declaring a method as virtual , you are stating your intention that the method can be overridden in a derived class. By declaring your implementing method as override , your are stating your intention that you are overriding a virtual method. By requiring that the override keyword be used to override a virtual method, the designers of the language encourage clarity, by requiring you to state your intentions.
