us.pca1$sdev are the square roots of the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix of the unscaled data matrix: > us.pca1$sdev [1] 83.732400 14.212402 6.489426 2.482790 > sqrt(eigen(var(USArrests))$values) [1] 83.732400 14.212402 6.489426 2.482790 whereas us.pca2$sdev are the square roots of the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix of the scaled data matrix, which are different because the scaled data has a different variance: > us.pca2$sdev [1] 1.5748783 0.9948694 0.5971291 0.4164494 > sqrt(eigen(var(scale(USArrests, center=TRUE, scale=TRUE)))$values) [1] 1.5748783 0.9948694 0.5971291 0.4164494 us.pca2$rotation is the new basis. The original data in coordinates with respect to this new basis is as.matrix(USArrests) %*% us.pca1$rotation . The standard deviations us.pca1$sdev are actually the standard deviations of the transformed data: > sqrt(apply(as.matrix(USArrests) %*% us.pca1$rotation, 2, var)) PC1 PC2 PC3 PC4 83.732400 14.212402 6.489426 2.482790
