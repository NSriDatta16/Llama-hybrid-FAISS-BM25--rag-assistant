in perl: my $work_url = $original_url; $work_url =~ s/\+/-/g; $url = '/blog/' . do { $work_url =~ m/\bstr=([\w\-]+)\b/; $1} . '/'; works for the example given. inspired by Ragepotato: $new_url = '/blog/' . sub { local $_ = shift; tr/+/-/; m/\bstr=([\w\-]+)\b/; $1 }->($orig_url) . '/'; And an stricter, less greedy regex for Ragepotatos post, untested: Regex.Match(input.Replace("+", "-"),@"\bstr=(.*?)&").Groups[1].Value
