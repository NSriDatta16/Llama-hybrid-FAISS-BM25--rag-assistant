Definitely not a complete answer, but just wanted to document the approach I took. A further understanding of CF and matrix factorization made me realise the 'rating' column represents not just a user-provided value, but how relevant an item is already. Recommendations are given without paying attention to an item features, just its rating I was able to calculate a new rating column (call it score), with a 0 to 100 value, that represented the sum of the user features I was after, hence resulting in what I was after I also opted to complement the results with a content-based recommender (different algorithm). This one provides another 0 to 100 scoring based on the amount of tag/attributes matched between an item an a user
