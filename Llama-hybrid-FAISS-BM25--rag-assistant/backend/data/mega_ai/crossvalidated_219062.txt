Stata has the margins command that makes this as easy as pie to get elasticities for continuous variables (% change in probability of each outcome for a % change in x) and semi-elasticities for dummy variables (% change in probability of each outcome when x goes from 0 to 1). mfx has been superseded by margins . Having said that, I find these elasticities hard to interpret and prefer to look at the change in probability for a % change in x or unit change in x. I will do that below as well, and try to connect the dots between the two ways of doing this.
