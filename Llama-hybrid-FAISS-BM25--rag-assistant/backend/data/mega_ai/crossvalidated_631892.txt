On the one hand, leverage domain knowledge to find good predictors. On the other hand, live with the fact that we never can predict as well as we would like to , and that even knowing when we have reached the limit of predictability can be hard . On the third hand, get clarity about what you want to predict, and tailor your analysis to this. This can entail cost-based learning, as Ggjj11 writes . Or it can mean selecting an objective function that is commensurate with what you are trying to do .
