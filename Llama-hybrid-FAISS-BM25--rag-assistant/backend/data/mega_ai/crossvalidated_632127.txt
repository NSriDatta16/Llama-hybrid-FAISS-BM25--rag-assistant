Let random vector ${ X \sim N(0, I _n) }$ . Its density is $f(x)= \frac{1}{(\sqrt{2\pi}) ^n} e ^{- \frac{\lVert x \rVert ^2}{2} } .$ So $$ \mathbb{P}( \lVert X \rVert \in [r,~ r + d r]) \approx C e ^{-r ^2 /2} r ^{n-1} ~d r .$$ Here $ e ^{-r ^2 /2} $ is density upto multiplicative constant, and $r ^{n-1} dr $ (i.e. derivative of $V _n (r) = r ^{n} V _n (1) $ times $d r $ ) is volume of shell upto multiplicative constant. Hence $$ \mathbb{P}(\lVert X \rVert \in [r, ~r + \Delta r]) = C \int _{r} ^{r + \Delta r} e ^{-u ^2/2} u ^{n-1} du,$$ where ${ C \int _{0} ^{\infty} e ^{-u ^2 /2} u ^{n-1} du = 1 }.$ We want $ r ,~ \Delta r $ so that $$ \mathbb{P}(\lVert X \rVert \in [r, ~r + \Delta r]) \approx 1 $$ i.e. $$\int _{r} ^{r + \Delta r} e ^{-u ^2/2} u ^{n-1} du \approx \int _{0}^{\infty} e ^{-u ^2 /2} u ^{n-1} du .$$ The function $e ^{-u ^2 /2} u ^{n-1} $ attains maximum at point where derivative $$ e ^{-u ^2 /2} (-u) u ^{n-1} + e ^{-u ^2/2} ((n-1) u ^{n-2}) = e ^{- u ^2 /2} u ^{n-2} (-u^2 + (n-1)) $$ is $0 .$ That is, maximum is attained at $ u = \sqrt{n-1} .$ So if at all most of the probability mass concentrates around a thin shell, it is around a shell of radius $r \approx \sqrt{n} .$ (The actual estimates showing Gaussian annulus theorem can be found for eg in Kannan, Hopcroft, Blum’s “ Foundations of data science ”).
