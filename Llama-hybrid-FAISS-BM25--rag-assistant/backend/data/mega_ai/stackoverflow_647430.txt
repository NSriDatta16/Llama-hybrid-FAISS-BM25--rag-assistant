It is the boolean algebra called " De Morgan's laws ". Not (P And Q) = (Not P) Or (Not Q) Not (P Or Q) = (Not P) And (Not Q) In your case, you want users not to enter 'n' or 'N' , it can be translate in to this logic. !(again == 'n' || again == 'N') When applying De Morgan's laws, it will be (again != 'n' && again != 'N') For more info, you might want to read Propositional logic
