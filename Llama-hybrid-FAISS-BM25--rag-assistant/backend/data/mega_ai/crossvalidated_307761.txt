I disagree with Tim in that the problem seems to me to fit within a Bayesian framework: Since $y∼ N(0,σ^2)$ is the mean of both observations, $x_1=y+ε_1$ and $x_2=y+ε_2$, where $ε_1∼ N(0,σ^2_{ε_1})$ and $ε_2∼ N(0,σ^2_{ε_2})$, the posterior distribution on the mean $y$ is with density $$f(y|x_1,x_2) \propto \exp\{-y^2/2σ^2\}\times \exp\{-(y-x_1)^2/2σ^2_{ε_1}\}\times \exp\{-(y-x_2)^2/2σ^2_{ε_2}\}$$which is a normal pdf on $y$ since \begin{align*}f(y|x_1,x_2) &\propto \exp\left\{-y^2/2[σ^{-2}+σ^{-2}_{ε_1}+σ^{-2}_{ε_2}] +y[σ^{-2}_{ε_1}x_1+σ^{-2}_{ε_2}x_2]\right\}\\ &\propto \exp\left\{-\frac{1}{2}\frac{(y-[σ^{-2}+σ^{-2}_{ε_1}+σ^{-2}_{ε_2}]^{-1}[σ^{-2}_{ε_1}x_1+σ^{-2}_{ε_2}x_2])^2}{σ^{-2}+\sigma^2_{ε_1}+σ^{-2}_{ε_2}}\right\} \end{align*} which means that the posterior on $y$ is a Gaussian distribution with mean $$\mathbb{E}[y|x_1,x_2]=[σ^{-2}+σ^{-2}_{ε_1}+σ^{-2}_{ε_2}]^{-1}[σ^{-2}_{ε_1}x_1+σ^{-2}_{ε_2}x_2]$$and variance $$\text{var}(y|x_1,x_2)=σ^{-2}+\sigma^2_{ε_1}+σ^{-2}_{ε_2}$$
