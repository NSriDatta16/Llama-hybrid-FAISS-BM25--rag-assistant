Is it fine to take the mean of what are median values? It is fine, from a mathematical view. The median of a sample or of a posterior distribution obtained from a sample is, like all statistics, just a random variable. There is nothing to say against taking its average. Inference from the average of medians may be mathematically more tricky, since theoretical properties are not so easy to derive than for averages of averages.
