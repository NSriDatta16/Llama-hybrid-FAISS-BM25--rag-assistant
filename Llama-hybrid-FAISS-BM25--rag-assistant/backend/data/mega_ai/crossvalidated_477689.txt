EDIT: It's not clear from your question how the authors of the mentioned article use the credibility (which is different from the credible interval of the Bayesian estimates). EDIT 2: After reading the article, the Bayesian credibility is defined setting a Normal prior and Normal likelihood. In this case, it coincides with Buhlmann credibility ( Jewell, W. (1974). ”Credible means are exact Bayesian for exponential families.” Astin Bulletin, 8, 1: 77–90. ) Still valid readings - if interested in Bayesian statistics and modelling - Good starting points are (not in a specific order): "Statistical Rethinking" by Richard McElreath, "A Student’s Guide to Bayesian Statistics" by Ben Lambert "Bayesian Data Analysis" by Andrew Gelman et al. From a practical point of view, I would suggest learning the R package brms .
