Breusch-Pagan-Godfrey and estimation of sigma

From what I know, we usually estimate $\hat{\sigma}^2 = \sum_{i=1}^n \frac{(y_i - \hat{y})^2}{n-p-1}$ . For the Breusch-Pagan-Godfrey test, my teacher told us to use $\hat{\sigma}^2 = \sum_{i=1}^n \frac{(y_i - \hat{y})^2}{n}$ . Why do we divide by n, and not n-p-1? ( $\hat{\sigma^2}$ is used to do the regression on $e_t = \frac{\hat{\epsilon}^2}{\hat{\sigma}^2}$ )
