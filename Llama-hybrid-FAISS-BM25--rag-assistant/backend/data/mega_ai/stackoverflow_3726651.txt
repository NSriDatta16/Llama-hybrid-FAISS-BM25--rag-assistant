You can absolutely use Azure Storage (which includes tables, blobs, and queues) with no compute instances. Storage costs $0.15 / GB $0.11 or less / GB depending on quantity, and you'll pay for bandwidth usage ( $0.10 free inbound, $0.15 $0.12 / GB outbound). And you'll pay $0.01 / 10,000 100,000 storage transactions. Regarding Azure tables specifically, you can have as many tables as you'd like within your storage account. Tables are schema-less, with up to 100TB per storage account. You can find more pricing info here . You can sign up for a 90-day trial, including storage, here .
