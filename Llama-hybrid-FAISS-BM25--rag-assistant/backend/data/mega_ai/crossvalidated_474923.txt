From a mixed-effects logistic regression, you can show/calculate two types of probabilities. Namely, conditional on the random effects or marginal populations. For more information on the difference between the two, check this post . Most often you want the latter. Regarding getting this in R, you can have a look at the effects package, and the GLMMadaptive package for both fitting the model and getting the plots. An example here .
