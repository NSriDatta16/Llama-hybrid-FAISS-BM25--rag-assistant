If you're going to localize them, I would use the English text as the key, and then perform a lookup into your storage of choice (an SQL-based database, file store, or what have you), and then return the properly localized string. Should the requested key not exist in the store, you could yield a plain-text error, perhaps e-mailing a developer? For more inspiration, see GNU gettext .
