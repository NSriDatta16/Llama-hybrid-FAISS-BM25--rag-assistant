The probability is the area (integral) under the PMF or PDF function, the likelihood is the value of the PMF or PDF function about the parameters. Bayesian inference uses data to make inferences about a parameter $\theta$ , $$f(\theta | x^n) = \frac{f(x^n | \theta) f(\theta)}{\int f(x^n | \theta) f(\theta) d\theta}$$ here the $f(\theta | x^n)$ is the likelihood. Let $C = (a, b)$ , the conditional probability (or posterior interval) is an integral $$\mathbb{P}(\theta \in C | x^n) = \int_a^b f(\theta | x^n) d\theta$$
