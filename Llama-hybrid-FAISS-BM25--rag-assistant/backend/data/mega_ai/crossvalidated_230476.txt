Question: Then if we want to be mathematically correct, shouldn't we disallow any interpretation of probability? I.e., are both Bayesian and frequentism mathematically incorrect? Yes, and this is exactly what people do both in Philosophy of Science and in Mathematics. Philosophical approach. Wikipedia provides a compendium of interpretations/definitions of probability . Mathematicians are not safe. In the past, the Kolmogorovian school had the monopoly of probability: a probability is defined as a finite measure that assigns 1 to the whole space ... This hegemony is no longer valid since there are new trends on defininig probability such as Quantum probability and Free probability .
