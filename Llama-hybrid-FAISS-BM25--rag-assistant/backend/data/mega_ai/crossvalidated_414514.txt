I found solution for my question. So I'm proposition an answer. 1- $Y_t$ is not always a markov chain. And it even be a Markov chain but not homogenus. 2- $\mathbb{P}[Y_{t+1} \neq A^2B, Y_t =A^2B,\dots, Y_1=A^2B| Y_0=A^2B] = \nu P_\mathcal{B}(I-P_\mathcal{B})\mathbf{1}$ where $\nu$ is the initial probability of $X_t$ on the set $\mathcal{B}:=\{AAB,ABA,BAA\}$ , $\mathbf{1} = \begin{bmatrix}1\\1\\1\end{bmatrix}$ and $P_\mathcal{B}$ is the transition probability on $\mathcal{B}$ in other that : $$P = \begin{bmatrix} P_\mathcal{B} & P_{\mathcal{B}\mathcal{B}^c}\\ P_{\mathcal{B}^c\mathcal{B}} & P_{\mathcal{B}^c}\end{bmatrix}$$ $\mathcal{B}^c = E_Y - \mathcal{B}$
