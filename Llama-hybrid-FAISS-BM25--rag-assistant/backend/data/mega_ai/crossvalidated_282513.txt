Bayesian inference: updating under uncertainty

Bayes Theorem: $$Pr(A|B) = \frac{Pr(A)Pr(B|A)}{Pr(B)} \tag{0}$$ Furthermore: $$Pr(A) = Pr(A|B)Pr(B) + Pr(A|\neg B)Pr(|\neg B) \tag{1}$$ Scenario: Suppose we are not given that an event $B$ has occurred. Assuming we are given a base Probability of $A$: $(Pr(A_1)$ (this is our prior probability), subsequently, we are now given a $Pr(A|B_1)$, a $Pr(B_1)$, a $Pr(A|\neg B_1)$, and a $Pr(\neg B_1)$ as additional information. Assuming, that I now want to calculate a $Pr(A_2)$ that is the posterior probability in light of the new information. From $(1)$: $$Pr(A_2) = Pr(A|B_1) Pr(B_1) + Pr(A|\neg B_1) Pr(\neg B_1)\tag{2}$$ From $(0)$: $$Pr(A|B_1) = \frac{Pr(A_1)Pr(B_1|A)}{Pr(B_1)} \qquad :\qquad Pr(A|\neg B_1) = \frac{Pr(A_1)Pr(\neg B_1|A)}{Pr(\neg B_1)} \tag{3}$$ Rewriting $(2)$ in light of $(3)$: $$Pr(A_2) = \frac{Pr(A_1)Pr(B_1|A)}{Pr(B_1)} \times Pr(B_1) + \frac{Pr(A_1)Pr(\neg B_1|A)}{Pr(\neg B_1)} \times Pr(\neg B_1)$$ Which gives us: $$Pr(A_2) = Pr(A_1)Pr(B_1|A) + Pr(A_1)Pr(\neg B_1|A)$$ Simplifying to: $$Pr(A_2) = Pr(A_1)\left(Pr(B_1|A) +Pr(\neg B_1|A)\right)\tag{4}$$ Generalising from $(4)$: $$Pr(A_{l+1}) = Pr(A_l)\left(Pr(B_l|A) +Pr(\neg B_l|A)\right)\tag{5}$$ My Question: Is $(5)$ valid Bayesian inference? If not, how do I perform valid inference in the specified scenario?
