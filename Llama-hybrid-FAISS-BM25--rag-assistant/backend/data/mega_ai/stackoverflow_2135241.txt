(Made an answer by request ;) Take a look at IERange : IERange is a feature-complete implementation of W3C DOM Ranges for Internet Explorer, allowing users to write one cross-browser version of their range manipulation code. Supports Range APIs: document.createRange() startContainer , startOffset , endContainer , endOffset , commonAncestorContainer , collapsed setStart() , setEnd() , setStartBefore() , setStartAfter() , setEndBefore() , setEndAfter() , selectNode() , selectNodeContents() , collapse() insertNode() , surroundContents() extractContents() , cloneContents() , deleteContents() compareBoundaryPoints() , cloneRange() , createContextualFragment() , toString() Supports Selection APIs: Range support (Webkit-style) window.getSelection() addRange() , removeAllRanges() , getRangeAt() , toString()
