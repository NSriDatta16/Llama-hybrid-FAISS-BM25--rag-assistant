Definitely, yes - many browsers already implement support for parts of HTML 5, or the features commonly associated with it, and the way some of them are, you won't break anything even if you did introduce them to a browser that didn't grok it. As always, you should use proper feature detection, rather than all-encompassing browser detection, to determine what's available - it's less vulnerable to broken User-Agent strings and the like. There's an excellent guide, Dive Into HTML 5 , which basically discusses this very point.
