Services are the way to go; a user doesn't have to be logged in for them to be running; they are well-equiped for remote administration; they are also better suited for being monitored for health Remoting can be used via WCF; WCF aggregates many different communication platforms and provides easy-to-leverage configurations for them. Learning WCF would be the better way to go, in my opinion, however if you are versed in Remoting and are certain it covers all your needs then it should be fine too.
