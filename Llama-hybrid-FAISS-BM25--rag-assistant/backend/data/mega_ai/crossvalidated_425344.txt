Derivation of posterior predictive distribution for autoregressive model

[Bayesian Choice ex. 1.37] If $x \sim N(\theta, \sigma^2)$ and $y \sim N(\rho x, \sigma^2)$ , as in an autoregressive mode, with $\rho$ known, and $\pi(\theta, \sigma^2) = 1/\sigma^2$ , give the predictive distribution of $y$ given $x$ . My attempt: $p(y|x) = \int \int f(y|x, \theta, \sigma)\pi(\theta, \sigma | x) d\theta d\sigma^2$ $\pi(\theta, \sigma | x) \propto \exp (-(\theta - x)^2/\sigma^2 )/\sigma^3 $ so $p(y|x) \propto \int \int \exp(-(y - \rho x)^2/2\sigma^2)\exp(-(x - \theta )^2/2\sigma^2)/\sigma^4 d\theta d\sigma^2 $ But how do you evaluate the integral? Update, $\int \exp(-(y - \rho x)^2/2\sigma^2)/\sigma^4 \underbrace{\int \exp(-(x - \theta )^2/2\sigma^2)d\theta}_{\propto \sigma} d\sigma^2 \propto$ $\int \exp(-(y - \rho x)^2/2\sigma^2)/\sigma^3 d\sigma^2 \propto$ $1 / (y - \rho x)$ , which is not proper
