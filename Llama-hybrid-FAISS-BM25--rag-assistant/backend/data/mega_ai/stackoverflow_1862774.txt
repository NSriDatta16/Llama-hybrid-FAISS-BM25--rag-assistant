Depending upon what you are doing with your multi-threading, this makes a difference. You are in a bank. Customers arrive at an average rate of 1 every 2 minutes. Each customer is served, on average, in 2 minutes. Which is the better solution to serving the customers? One common line, or one line for each teller? Is your choice enough to guarantee some bound on the length of the line? Answers: because of the markov property of customer arrival and actual service time per individual, the line will never know a bound. additionally, it's a good idea to make them wait in one common line, although this is not enough to overcome the boundless line.
