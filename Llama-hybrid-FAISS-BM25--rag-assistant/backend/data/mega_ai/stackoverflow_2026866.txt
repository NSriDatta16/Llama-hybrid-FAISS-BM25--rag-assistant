Practically, with 1-bit coded CMYK you have 16 possible combinations (colors), 9 of which are black. xxx1 -- black (8 comb's) -- 0x000000 0000 -- white -- 0xFFFFFF 1000 -- cyan -- 0x00FFFF 0100 -- magenta -- 0xFF00FF 0010 -- yellow -- 0xFFFF00 1100 -- blue -- 0x0000FF 1010 -- green -- 0x00FF00 0110 -- red -- 0xFF0000 1110 -- black -- 0x000000 Given this, I'd rather go with a mapping (switch-case or if-elses) than an actual algorithm for calculating RGB from CMYK. Of course, return early if K is set.
