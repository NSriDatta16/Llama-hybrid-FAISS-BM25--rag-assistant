Is there any reason why this might be the case or is it unavoidable? There are a few possible reasons why this might be the case, and they are unavoidable. Here are two that pop to mind: While you may think there are "no additional programs running in the background", the operating system may disagree with you If you are reading data from flash storage, access times may be variable due to the peculiarities of YAFFS2 (e.g., global lock for an entire partition) Also, do not forget that device CPU and flash speeds will vary themselves. While your device is, um, on the slow end, there may be some that are slower still, and there will be many that are faster.
