xgboost.train will ignore parameter n_estimators , while xgboost.XGBRegressor accepts. In xgboost.train , boosting iterations (i.e. n_estimators ) is controlled by num_boost_round (default: 10) In your case, the first code will do 10 iterations (by default), but the second one will do 1000 iterations. There won't be any big difference if you try to change clf = xg.train(params, dmatrix) into clf = xg.train(params, dmatrix, 1000) , References http://xgboost.readthedocs.io/en/latest/python/python_api.html#xgboost.train http://xgboost.readthedocs.io/en/latest/python/python_api.html#xgboost.XGBRegressor
