Question about expanding a vector form of covariance of two r.v

I am trying to proof why the two lines of the following expression are equal. I found it in one of the books on machine learning $$ \begin{align} \operatorname{Cov}[\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}] &= \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}}\bigl[ \{\mathbf{x} - \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x}}{[\mathbf{x}]}\} \{\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}} - \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{y}}\{\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}]\} \bigr] \\ &= \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}}\bigl[\mathbf{x}\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}\bigr] - \mathbb{E}{[\mathbf{x}]}\mathbb{E}[\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}]. \end{align} $$ If I expand the first line, the cross terms disappear. It is not explained in the book, so I tried to work it out by myself. Here's the approach: $$ \begin{align} \operatorname{Cov}[\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}] &= \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}}\bigl[ \{\mathbf{x} - \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x}}{[\mathbf{x}]}\} \{\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}} - \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{y}}\{\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}]\} \bigr] \\ &= \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}}\bigl[ \mathbf{x}\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}} - \mathbf{x}\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{y}}[\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}] - \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x}}[\mathbf{x}]\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}} + \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x}}{[\mathbf{x}]}\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{y}}{[\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}]} \bigr] \\ &= \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}}\bigl[\mathbf{x}\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}\bigr] - \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}}\bigl[\mathbf{x}\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{y}}[\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}]\bigr] - \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}}\bigl[\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x}}[\mathbf{x}]\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}\bigr] + \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}}\bigl[\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x}}{[\mathbf{x}]}\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{y}}{[\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}]}\bigr] \\ &= \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}}\bigl[\mathbf{x}\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}\bigr] - \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x}}\bigl[\mathbf{x}\bigr]\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{y}}[\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}] - \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x}}[\mathbf{x}]\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{y}}\bigl[\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}\bigr] + \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x}}{[\mathbf{x}]}\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{y}}{[\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}]} \\ &= \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}}\bigl[\mathbf{x}\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}\bigr] - \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x}}{[\mathbf{x}]}\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{y}}[\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}]. \end{align} $$ I understand expectation of a constant is the constant itself. Hence, exp. from another expectation is the expectation. I am not 100% sure about each middle term, s.a. $$ \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}}\bigl[\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x}}[\mathbf{x}]\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}\bigr]\ \equiv\ \mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x}}[\mathbf{x}]\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{y}}\bigl[\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}\bigr]\quad\text{or simply}\quad\mathbb{E}[\mathbf{x}]\mathbb{E}\bigl[\mathbf{y}^{\mathrm{T}}\bigr] $$ If that is not correct, I wonder what makes the first expression true? Thanks.
