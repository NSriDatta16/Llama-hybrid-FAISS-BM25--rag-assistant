...for the Pragmatic programmer ;-) The `#pragma' directive is the method specified by the C standard for providing additional information to the compiler, beyond what is conveyed in the language itself. Three forms of this directive (commonly known as pragmas) are specified by the 1999 C standard. A C compiler is free to attach any meaning it likes to other pragmas. http://gcc.gnu.org/onlinedocs/cpp/Pragmas.html http://msdn.microsoft.com/en-us/library/d9x1s805%28VS.71%29.aspx
