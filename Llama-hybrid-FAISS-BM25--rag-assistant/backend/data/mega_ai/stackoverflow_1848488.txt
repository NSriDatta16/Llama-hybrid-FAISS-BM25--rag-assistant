Chapter 6 of "Practical API Design" by Jaroslav Tulach is titled "Code Against Interfaces, Not Implementations". It explains that, by coding against an interface rather than a specific implementation, you can decouple modules (or components) in a system and therefore raise the system quality. Bertrand Meyer in OOSC2 explains clearly why "closing" a system and making it more modular raises its quality.
