The behavior is related to the as keyword in C#. A Dictionary is not the same thing as an IDictionary. If you were casting the other way, you may be able to get it to work in the next version of .NET which has increased support for covariance and contravariance . The solution you might want to determine why you need to cast to the concrete Dictionary/List, and if it's required, then change the storage of the type.
