Nothing surprising about this. The standard error of an estimator diminishes with a square root of the sample size. So if you take more measurements and average them, you would always be better than with fewer samples. The same phenomenon was observed outside of statistics, James Surowiecki described in his book the wisdom of the crowd , who quotes many experiments where averaging opinions of many people, even if lacking expertise, often gave better results than the predictions by experts.
