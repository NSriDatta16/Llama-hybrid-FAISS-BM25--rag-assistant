Most common clustering approaches require a distance metric to be defined between your samples. Essentially, how do you know that two samples are similar and should be in the same cluster? Some starting points for defining such metrics could be Alternate distance metrics for two time series or https://stackoverflow.com/questions/48497756/time-series-distance-metric Once you have an appropriate distance metric, most common clustering algorithms should work "out-of-the-box" in the same way as they would work on non-time-series data.
