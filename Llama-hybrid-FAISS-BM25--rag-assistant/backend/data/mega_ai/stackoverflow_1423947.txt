If you are coming from Groovy background where they have an implicit iterator, or even from Scala where you can do without this |i| using partial functions, then you might find it a logical question, however, at the current moment the best you can do in Ruby 1.9 or even inside Rails, is to use the Symbol#to_block method like JRL mentioned: p test_method &:upcase So just add the &: before the method name.
