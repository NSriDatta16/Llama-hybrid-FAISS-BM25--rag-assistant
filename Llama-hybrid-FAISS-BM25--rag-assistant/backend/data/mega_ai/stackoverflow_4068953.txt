+1 TO Amber. The alternative syntax is no doubt more readable, especially when inserting control structures among HTML. And yes, the accepted coding standards varies on the organization. In our case, we use the if(): endif in templates, and if(){} in logic and backend portion of the code. Observe the neatness of the code, including the indentation: I guess the number is odd I guess the number is even In this case, you don't even need that pesky semicolon in the end. Just to compare, here is the "unalternative" (or original) way: I guess the number is odd I guess the number is even Observe the aesthetically-disturbing curly braces all over the code. The first style fits better with HTML tags.
