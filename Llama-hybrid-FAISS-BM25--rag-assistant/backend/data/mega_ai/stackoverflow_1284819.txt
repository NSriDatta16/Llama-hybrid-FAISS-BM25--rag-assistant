This is an old problem, DWIM (Do What I Mean), famously implemented on the Xerox Alto by Warren Teitelman. If your problem is based on pronunciation, here is a survey paper that might help: J. Zobel and P. Dart, "Phonetic String Matching: Lessons from Information Retieval," Proc. 19th Annual Inter. ACM SIGIR Conf. on Research and Development in Information Retrieval (SIGIR'96) , Aug. 1996, pp. 166-172. I'm told by my friends who work in information retrieval that Soundex as described by Knuth is now considered very outdated.
