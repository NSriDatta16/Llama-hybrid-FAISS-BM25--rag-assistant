Use a boxcar filter. The problem with survival methods is that they estimate hazards which have to be transformed to risks and, at times, rely on parametric assumptions. Since you have no censoring in these data, the mean is an unbiased estimator of the counting process. The boxcar filter provides a nonparametric moving average of the frequency of events at each time and is a nice descriptive statistic. An example of my superinefficient approach: data(cancer) window
