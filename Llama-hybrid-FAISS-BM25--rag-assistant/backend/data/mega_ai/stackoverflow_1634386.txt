, and %q{} are your friends. Info on using them from the Programming Ruby The Pragmatic Programmer's Guide . Try mixing the various approaches: %q{`~!@#$%^&*()_-+}+"{}"+%q{=|[]\\:";'<>?,./} or alternatively, just use backslashes to escape the problematic chars: "`~!@\#$%^&*()_-+{}=|[]\\:\";'<>?,./"
