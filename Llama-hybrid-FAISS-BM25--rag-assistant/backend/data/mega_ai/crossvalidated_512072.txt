Time series, linear process, white noise

Let $X_n = \sum\limits_{j=0}^{\infty} a_jZ_{t-j}$ , where $Z_t$ is a (weak) white noise $(0,\sigma^2)$ and $a_j \in L^2$ . Prove that ACF $\gamma_X(h)\longrightarrow 0$ as $h\longrightarrow +\infty$ . So: $\gamma_X(h)=\text{Cov}(X_{t+h},X_t)=\text{Cov}(\sum\limits_{i=0}^{\infty} a_iZ_{t+h-i}, \sum\limits_{j=0}^{\infty}a_jZ_{t-j})=\lim\limits_{n,m\to\infty} \text{Cov}(\sum\limits_{i=0}^{n} a_iZ_{t+h-i}, \sum\limits_{j=0}^{m}a_jZ_{t-j})\overset{\textbf{if true: why?}}{=} \lim\limits_{n,m\to\infty}\sum\limits_{i=0}^{n}\sum\limits_{j=0}^{m} \text{Cov}(a_iZ_{t+h-i}, a_jZ_{t-j})$ and if we have weak white noise, then only non-zero terms of $\text{Cov}$ above are these that satisfy $t+h-i=t-j \longleftrightarrow h=i-j$ , but... how to proceed from that point?
