Your last statement is definitely not true in general. For example, conversation bots are still not very smart. They are infrequently capable of having small talk, let alone passing a Turing test with a competent judge or answering general knowledge questions. Watson is something, but there's still a long way to go. Identifying what's in a picture is another problem that's far from solved despite getting a lot of attention and some exciting advances. Getting back to everyday uses of machine learning and statistics, the usual things that can tip you off right out of the gate that you probably won't get anywhere are: not much data being available, not much variability in the IVs, very wide data, etc.
