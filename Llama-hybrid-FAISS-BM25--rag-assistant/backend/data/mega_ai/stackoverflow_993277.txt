I'm new in R, but if you want to draw scale vs. all other columns in one plot, easy and with some elegance :) for printing or presentation, you may use Prof. Hadley Wickham's packages ggplot2 & reshape. Installation: install.packages(“ggplot2”,dep=T) install.packages(“reshape”,dep=T) Drawing your example: library(ggplot2) library(reshape) #read data data = read.table("foo.csv", header=T,sep=",") #melt data “scale vs. all” data2=melt(data,id=c("scale")) data2 scale variable value 1 5 serial 0.000178 2 10 serial 0.156986 3 12 serial 2.658998 4 15 serial 188.023411 5 5 spawn 0.000288 6 10 spawn 0.297926 7 12 spawn 6.059502 8 15 spawn 719.463264 9 5 for. 0.000292 10 10 for. 0.064509 11 12 for. 0.912733 12 15 for. 164.111459 13 5 worker 0.000300 14 10 worker 0.066297 15 12 worker 0.923606 16 15 worker 161.687982 #draw all variables at once as line with different linetypes qplot(scale,value,data=data2,geom="line",linetype=variable) You could also use points ( geom=”points” ), choose different colours or shapes for different variables dots ( colours=variable or shape=variable ), adjust axis, set individual options for every line etc. Link to online documentation .
