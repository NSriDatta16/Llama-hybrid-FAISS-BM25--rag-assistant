Does the variance of regression coefficient in multiple linear regression increases if we replace one of the predictors with its immediate parent?

We have a data generation process as follows: \begin{align} Z_1 &= E_{Z_1}\\ Z_2 &= \theta_{Z_2} + \theta_{Z_1 Z_2} Z_1 + E_{Z_2} \label{Z2BasedOnZ1}\\ T &= \theta_{T} + \theta_{Z_1 T} Z_1 + E_{T} \\ M_1 &= \theta_{M_1} + { \theta_{{T}{M_1}} } T + E_{M_1} \\ M_2 &= \theta_{M_2} + { \theta_{{M_1}{M_2}} }M_1 + E_{M_2} \\ Y &= \theta_{Y} + { \theta_{{M_2}{Y}} } M_2 + \theta_{{Z_2}{Y}}Z_2 + E_Y \end{align} We assume that $E_{Z_1}$ , $E_{Z_2}$ , $E_{T}$ , $E_{M_1}$ , $E_{M_2}$ , and $E_Y$ are independent from each other, and come from $N(0,\sigma^2)$ . We are interested in estimating the average causal effect (ACE) of $T$ on $Y$ , and estimating the variance of ACE. Case 1: All the variables are observed: The estimate of ACE and its variance is defined as: \begin{align} \widehat{ACE}(t) &= \hat{E}[Y|do(t+1)] - \hat{E}[Y|do(t)] = \hat{\theta}_{{T}{M_1}} \hat{\theta}_{{M_1}{M_2}} \hat{\theta}_{{M_2}{Y}}\\ {var}(\widehat{ACE}(t)) &= {var}(\hat{E}[Y|do(t+1)] - \hat{E}[Y|do(t)]) = var(\hat{\theta}_{{T}{M_1}} \hat{\theta}_{{M_1}{M_2}} \hat{\theta}_{{M_2}{Y}}) \end{align} Case 2: $Z_2$ is latent: In this case, in the last equation, instead of regressing $M_2$ and $Z_2$ on $Y$ , we should regress $M_2$ and $Z_1$ on $Y$ . Hence our equations become: \begin{align} Z_1 &= E_{Z_1}\\ T &= \theta_{T} + \theta_{Z_1 T} Z_1 + E_{T} \\ M_1 &= \theta_{M_1} + { \theta_{{T}{M_1}} } T + E_{M_1} \\ M_2 &= \theta_{M_2} + { \theta_{{M_1}{M_2}} }M_1 + E_{M_2} \\ Y &= \theta'_{Y} + { \theta'_{{M_2}{Y}} } M_2 + \theta'_{{Z_1}{Y}}Z_1 + E'_Y \end{align} The estimate of ACE and its variance are: \begin{align} \widehat{ACE}(t) &= \hat{E}[Y|do(t+1)] - \hat{E}[Y|do(t)] = \hat{\theta}_{{T}{M_1}} \hat{\theta}_{{M_1}{M_2}} \hat{\theta}'_{{M_2}{Y}}\\ {var}(\widehat{ACE}(t)) &= {var}(\hat{E}[Y|do(t+1)] - \hat{E}[Y|do(t)]) = var(\hat{\theta}_{{T}{M_1}} \hat{\theta}_{{M_1}{M_2}} \hat{\theta}'_{{M_2}{Y}}) \end{align} How can we compare $var(\hat{\theta}_{{T}{M_1}} \hat{\theta}_{{M_1}{M_2}} \hat{\theta}'_{{M_2}{Y}})$ with $var(\hat{\theta}_{{T}{M_1}} \hat{\theta}_{{M_1}{M_2}} \hat{\theta}_{{M_2}{Y}})$ ? I expect that $var(\hat{\theta}_{{T}{M_1}} \hat{\theta}_{{M_1}{M_2}} \hat{\theta}'_{{M_2}{Y}})$ > $var(\hat{\theta}_{{T}{M_1}} \hat{\theta}_{{M_1}{M_2}} \hat{\theta}_{{M_2}{Y}})$ , because $Z_2$ is the immediate parent of $Y$ and by replacing it with $Z_1$ , we are reducing certainty about $Y$ , and hence increasing the variance. Any thoughts?
