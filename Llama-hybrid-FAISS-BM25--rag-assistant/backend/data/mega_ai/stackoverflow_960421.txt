I believe the point here is not on what the system does, but on the data it operates on and what those operations are. This provides two major thinking shifts: You think of the data and concepts first You think of operations on that data With those two "baselines" you will better prepared to organize a system to achieve your goals so that operations on data are well understood and make sense. In effect, he is laying the ground work to be able to write the "contracts" on the code you write.
