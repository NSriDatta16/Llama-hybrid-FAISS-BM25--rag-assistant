1) When you have to interchange your data with others. XML is the " lingua franca " of the Web -- just about everyone can read and interpret it, unlike a database file. 2) When your data volume is small and you don't have to do complex queries against it. XML files are good for things like storing configuration or document templates. 3) When you don't have many writers trying to access the same data. SQL databases have involved concurrency mechanisms that are working behind the scenes for you. SQL databases can support indexes for the retrieval of information on large data sets quickly...
