That's because ios places your animating view to the target position, when the animation starts, but draws it on the path. So if you tap the view while moving, you actually tap somewhere out of its frame. In your animating view's init, set userInteractionEnabled to NO. So the touch events are handled by the superview. self.userInteractionEnabled = NO; In your superview's touchesBegan method, check your animating view's presentationLayer position.
