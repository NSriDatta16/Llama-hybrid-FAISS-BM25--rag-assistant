The effect estimate is the difference in the result from when your factor changes from the high value to the low value. In your problem statement above the effect estimate for factor A is the average etch rate when factor A=1 minus the average etch rate when A=-1. When A= 1: Average( 669, 650, 642, 635, 749, 868, 729, 860)= 725.25 When A= -1: Average( 550, 604, 633, 601, 1037, 1052, 1075, 1063)= 826.875 Thus the effect estimate for factor A = 727.25 - 826.875 = -101.625. Now repeat for the remaining factors and for the interaction factors. In R, here is the script: et_rate = c(550, 669, 633, 642, 1037, 749, 1075, 729, 604, 650, 601, 635, 1052, 868, 1063, 860) gap = (rep(c(-1, 1), times = 8)) flw_rate = (rep(c(-1, 1), each = 2, times = 4)) pwr = (rep(c(-1, 1), each = 4, times= 2)) df $AB gap*df $flw_rate df$ AC $gap*df$ pwr df $BC flw_rate*df $pwr df$ ABC $gap*df$ flw_rate*df$pwr variables $et_rate[df[[var]]==1] - df$ et_rate[df[[var]]==-1]) }) #Output: gap flw_rate pwr AB AC BC ABC -101.625 7.375 306.125 -24.875 -153.625 -2.125 5.625 #perform the ANOVA summary(aov(et_rate ~ gap*flw_rate*pwr, data=df)) Df Sum Sq Mean Sq F value Pr(>F) gap 1 41311 41311 18.339 0.002679 ** flw_rate 1 218 218 0.097 0.763911 pwr 1 374850 374850 166.411 1.23e-06 *** gap:flw_rate 1 2475 2475 1.099 0.325168 gap:pwr 1 94403 94403 41.909 0.000193 *** flw_rate:pwr 1 18 18 0.008 0.930849 gap:flw_rate:pwr 1 127 127 0.056 0.818586 Residuals 8 18020 2253 --- Signif. codes: 0 ‘***’ 0.001 ‘**’ 0.01 ‘*’ 0.05 ‘.’ 0.1 ‘ ’ 1 This the reason why the levels are generally defined as -1 and 1!
