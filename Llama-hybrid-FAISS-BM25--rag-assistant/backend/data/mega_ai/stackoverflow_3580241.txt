Without knowing what specific .NET technology you have access to, it is difficult to be sure of an answer, however, you may find this helpful. If you are serving the content in ASP.NET, you can set the content disposition and content type to encourage correct treatment from the browser: Response.AppendHeader("content-disposition", "attachment; filename=myexe.exe"); Response.ContentType = "application/octet-stream"; Here is an old, but reasonably applicable example online of controlling the HttpResonse in ASP.NET: http://aspalliance.com/259
