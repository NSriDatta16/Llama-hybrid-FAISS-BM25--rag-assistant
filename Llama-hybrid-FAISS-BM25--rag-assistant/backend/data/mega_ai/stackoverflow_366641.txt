You can use a little bash script; name it "popcmd": #!/bin/bash cmd=`head -n 1 $1` tail -n +2 $1 > ~tmp~ mv -f ~tmp~ $1 $cmd edit: Using sed for the middle two lines, like Charlie Martin showed, is much more elegant, of course: #!/bin/bash cmd=`head -n 1 $1` sed -i -e '1d' $1 $cmd edit: You can use this exactly as in your example usage code: proc_A | proc_B | popcmd cmdstack.lst | proc_D
