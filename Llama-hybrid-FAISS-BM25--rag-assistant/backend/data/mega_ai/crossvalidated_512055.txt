Linear process autocovariance function converges to zero as h goes to infinity

Assume that time series $(X_t)$ is given by: \begin{equation} X_t = \sum_{i=0}^{\infty} c_i \varepsilon_{t - i}, \end{equation} where $(\varepsilon_t)$ is a weak white noise $\text{WN}(0, \sigma^2)$ and sequence $(c_n)$ converges in $l^2$ . How to prove, that the autocovariance function: \begin{equation} \gamma_X(h) = Cov(X_{t+h}, X_t) \end{equation} converges to zero, as $h \rightarrow \infty$ ?
