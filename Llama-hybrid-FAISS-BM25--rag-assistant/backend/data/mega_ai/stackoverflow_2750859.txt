If this interacts with a database, you should get a feel for Disk I/O and the degree of fragmentation of the disk array / hard drive. For MS SQL, analyze any stored procedures and review the indexes and primary keys on the tables. You really do not needs tools for this, just the grunt work of reviewing counters and talking with the DBA .
