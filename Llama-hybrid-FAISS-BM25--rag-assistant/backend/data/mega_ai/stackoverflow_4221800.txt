>>> r = re.compile(r'(\s*)((?:\s*\b(?:iphone|games|mac)\b)+)', re.I) >>> r.sub(r'\1 \2 ', sentence) 'I love downloading iPhone games from my mac .' The extra group fully containing the plus-repetition avoids losing words, while shifting the spaces before the words — but taking out leading spaces initially — handles that problem. The word boundary assertions require full word matching for the 3 words between them. However, NLP is hard and there will still be cases where this doesn't work as expected.
