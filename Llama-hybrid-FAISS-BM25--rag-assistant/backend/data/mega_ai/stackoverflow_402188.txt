As Martin Fowler suggests, do it once, fine. do it twice, starts to smell. do it thrice, time to refactor . EDIT: in answer to the comment, the origin of the advice is Don Roberts: Three strikes and you refactor . Martin Fowler describes that in Refactoring chapter 2, section The Rule of Three (page 58).
