An Ada pragma is a language feature that allows adjusting or fine-tuning the behavior of an Ada program. A number of pragmas are defined by the Ada language standard, but compiler vendors can also define their own. The subject of pragmas cover far more than just process [tasking] priorities, here's a list of standard and vendor-provided pragmas to check out. What exactly are you trying to do with the aid of pragmas? Set task priorities? -- Not compile checked: with System; use System; task Prioritized_Task is pragma Priority(System.Default_Priority + 1); entry Start; end Prioritized_Task;
