PCA is different to EFA. In PCA, we don't have a hypothesis of the underlying structure of the data. We use PCA to simplify the dimension of the data, for each eigenvector. In EFA, we have a number of latent constructs which we want to run the analysis. Apart from the overall goal, the analysis and calculation is very similar. In the paper, I think the authors performed a PCA with a rotation for the factors to improve the interpretation of the components. To quote @ttnphns in his answer on PCA vs FA : PCA: rotation/interpretation of components - sometimes
