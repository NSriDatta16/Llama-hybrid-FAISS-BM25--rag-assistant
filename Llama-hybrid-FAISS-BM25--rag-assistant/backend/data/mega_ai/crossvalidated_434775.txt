Suppose $Y$ is binary. The parameters are as follows: \begin{align*} \pi_1 &\equiv \Pr(Y=1\mid X_1=0, X_2=0, X_3=0), \\ \pi_2 &\equiv \Pr(Y=1\mid X_1=0, X_2=0, X_3=1), \\ \pi_3 &\equiv \Pr(Y=1\mid X_1=0, X_2=1, X_3=0), \\ &\vdots \\ \pi_8 &\equiv \Pr(Y=1\mid X_1=1, X_2=1, X_3=1). \end{align*} So the parameters are the success probabilities under each combination of coin flips. A nonprametric estimator slices the sample up into these eight subsamples and computes the average of $Y$ in each subsample to get $\hat\pi_1, \ldots, \hat\pi_8$ .
