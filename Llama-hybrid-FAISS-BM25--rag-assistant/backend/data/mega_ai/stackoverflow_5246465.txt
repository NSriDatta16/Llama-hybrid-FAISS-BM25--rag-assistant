It is a in-document link. It's called anchor (or fragment) and it is used to link to certain parts within a page and have the browser directly scroll to the right part of the page. Try this (and read the contents :-)) http://www.w3.org/TR/html4/struct/links.html#h-12.2.3 This browser mechanism has recently been exploited to perform certain advanced javascript/ajax tricks and it's being used extensively by facebook, twitter, etc. When doing so a general convention is to append a ! after the # to distinguish a javascript-bound anchor from a normal one.
