You have the tag "Bayesian", so I just thought I'd mention the centrality of the posterior distribution $P(B|A)$ (where $B$ is an unknown parameter and $A$ the data) to Bayesian inference. In other words, it's because $P(B|A)$ tends to be the thing we care about.
