How to interpret mean squared error matrix in the context of multiple time series?

Consider the time series of 2 variables $x_1$ and $x_2$, put together $y=(x_1,x_2)$: $$ y_t = \begin{bmatrix} v_1\\ v_2 \end{bmatrix} +\begin{bmatrix} c & d\\ e & f \end{bmatrix}y_{t-1} + u_t. $$ The fixed, nonsingular covaraince matrix is: $\Sigma_u = \begin{bmatrix} \sigma_1^{2} & 0\\ 0 & \sigma_2^{2} \end{bmatrix}$. The mean squared error matrix of the 1-step ahead forecasts is hence also: $\begin{bmatrix} \sigma_1^{2} & 0\\ 0 & \sigma_2^{2} \end{bmatrix}$. How should I interpret this mean squared error matrix?
