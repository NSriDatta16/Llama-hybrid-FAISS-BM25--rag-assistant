By way of answering this, I'd direct you to a book that does quite a good job it: Professional ASP.NET Design Patterns by Scott Millett. The book takes the most popular patterns, including those documented by the "Gang of Four" and the enterprise patterns of Martin Fowler, and discusses them in terms of all ASP.NET app types, including MVC. If he can't convince you that MVC offers advantages in partitioning user display, business logic, and data store, probably no one will
