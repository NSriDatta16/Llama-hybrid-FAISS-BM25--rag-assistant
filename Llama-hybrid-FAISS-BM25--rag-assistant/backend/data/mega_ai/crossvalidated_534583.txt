I think that, $$ X_3 = \cases{X_1, V = 0\\X_2, V = 1} $$ Therefore, $$X_3 \sim N(\theta, 1)$$ Check if $X_3$ is independent of $X_1$ and $X_2$ . So what you have presented is the average of 3 iid observations of the variable $X \sim N(\theta, 1)$ . It is known that $\bar{x} \sim N(\theta, \frac{\sigma}{n})$ . Which in this case $\bar{x} \sim N(\theta, \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}})$ . So I reject $H_0$ if $P(\bar{x} > c) = P(Z > \frac{c - \theta }{1/\sqrt{3}}) = P(Z > \sqrt{3}(c - \theta)) = 0.05$ . It follows then that $$c - \theta = \frac{1.64}{\sqrt{3}} \Rightarrow c = \theta + \frac{1.64}{\sqrt{3}}$$
