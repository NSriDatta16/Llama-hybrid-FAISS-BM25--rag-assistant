This is something I would leave up to the user, allowing then to actually make a choice. All the UIs I've seen for allowing user-specified conditions have: the column to check. a drop-down box containing the relationship, such as equal to , not equal to , less than , greater than , starts with . the value you want to compare to. Then, for the starts with option, you just tack on % and use like . You'll note (for performance reasons which you seem to already understand) I used starts with rather than like to limit the possibility of dragging down the database performance. I'm not a big fan of unrestricted like statements although you could also provide ends with for those DBMS' capable of storing reversed indexes.
