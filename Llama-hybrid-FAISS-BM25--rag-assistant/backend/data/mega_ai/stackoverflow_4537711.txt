I can think of 2 reasons for this behaviour: the signal itemDoubleClicked is handled somewhere in your QListWidget and does something unintendet, or your "more code" in dropEvent does something bad when source and destination are the same (you could check if startRow is equal to endRow and do nothing in this case). Edit: Does this program work for you: #include #include int main(int argc, char **argv) { QApplication a(argc, argv); QListWidget lw; for(int i = 1; i setAcceptDrops(true); // *** lw.setDefaultDropAction(Qt::MoveAction); // *** lw.setDropIndicatorShown(true); // *** lw.setDragDropMode(QAbstractItemView::InternalMove); lw.show(); a.connect(&a, SIGNAL(lastWindowClosed()), &a, SLOT(quit())); a.exec(); } Lines with three stars can be deleted. This program works for me on Windows XP with Qt 4.7.1, compiled with VS2010.
