This may sound not very PC, but I believe that software engineering is not a religion which would force you to observe strict precepts just for the sake of it. There are, of course, theoretical explanations for Dos and Donts , there are tons of Considered Harmful essays, but do they always apply to your own case ? Let's just be pragmatic: Your MembershipException class is specialized enough, and most of all, it is easy to maintain with it's MembershipError enum. Also, the cost of exception handling is often overrated: your membership service layer is not a real-time flight simulator after all; a login failure once in a while will not put your application to its knees. Just keep it like this: simple to maintain and simple to read.
