They are useful if you view your users as data entry clerks to maintain your database tables for you. They help minimize the programming time required to meet minimum requirements. If you want the quality of your work to reflect something better than that, the best that can be said for them is they might give you a jumpstart if you're not too sure how to do simple consistent UI screens yourself. Personally I find that refactoring them into something useful and attractive based on real Use Cases takes longer than doing it from scratch. They're the kind of technique Dilbert's pointy-haired boss would love.
