Since $X$ and $\epsilon$ are supposed to be independent and so uncorrelated, you can subtract the means, square and average to give $\sigma^2_Y = b^2 \sigma^2_X + \sigma^2_\epsilon$. But $b^2 \sigma^2_X = \rho_{X,Y}^2 \sigma^2_Y$ so you can substitute and rearrange to get your result.
