There are three tests of nested regression models that get covered in a typical statistical theory class, such as one taught from the Casella/Berger textbook Statistical Inference . Wald test Likelihood ratio test Score test (sometimes called a “Lagrange multiplier test”) In linear regression, the F-test is an example of a Wald test. When you get to logistic regression, however, there are issues with Wald testing. Consequently, there are arguments to be made that likelihood ratio testing is more appropriate. That’s what your printout is giving.
