Bound for the bias of ergodic averages for non-stationary Markov chains

Let $(\Omega,\mathcal A,\operatorname P)$ be a probability space $(\mathcal F_n)_{n\in\mathbb N_0}$ be a filtration on $(\Omega,\mathcal A)$ $(E,\mathcal E)$ be a measurable space $X$ be a $(E,\mathcal E)$ -valued time-homogeneous $\mathcal F$ -Markov chain on $(\Omega,\mathcal A,\operatorname P)$ with transition kernel $\kappa$ , initial distribution $\nu$ and stationary distribution $\mu$ $f\in\mathcal L^1(\mu)$ and $$A_{b,\:n}f:=\frac1n\sum_{i=b}^{b+n-1}f(X_i)$$ for some $b\in\mathbb N_0$ and $n\in\mathbb N$ I'm interested in the bias of the estimator $A_{b,\:n}f$ of $$\mu f:=\int f\:{\rm d}\mu.$$ Actually, if $f$ is bounded, then it's not hard to show that $$\left|\operatorname E\left[A_{b,\:n}f\right]-\mu f\right|\le\left|\nu\kappa^b-\mu\right|\sup_{x\in E}\left|f_0(x)\right|\tag1,$$ where $\left|\nu\kappa^b-\mu\right|$ is the total variation distance of $\nu\kappa^b$ and $\mu$ and $f_0:=f-\mu f$ . Boundedness in $(1)$ is already needed in order to ensure that $\operatorname E\left[A_{b,\:n}f\right]$ is well-defined. We know that $$\operatorname P\circ X_n=\underbrace{\left(\operatorname P\circ X_0\right)}_{=\:\nu}\kappa^n.\tag2$$ Is it somehow possible to drop the boundedness assumption in $(1)$ , i.e. is there a similar statement for a general $f\in\mathcal L^1(\mu)$ ? Clearly, the question becomes trivial whenver $\mu$ is invariant with respect to $\kappa$ (since then $\operatorname P\circ X_n=\mu=\nu$ ). Remark : In my application, $X$ is generated by the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm and I'm trying to figure out how "good" the approximation $A_{b,\:n}f$ of $\mu f$ is.
