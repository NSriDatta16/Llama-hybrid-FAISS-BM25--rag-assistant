Allocating larger chunks is commonly done in pool allocators, where the overhead of allocation and deallocation is reduced and locality is increased (as memory is more likely to be consecutive). More information on pool allocators In many cases, fragmentation is your worst enemy. When you are allocating, keep object sizes consistent or in sizes (power of two is popular, but may be too wasteful). This reduces fragmentation as there are only a few common sizes of memory which are allocated.
