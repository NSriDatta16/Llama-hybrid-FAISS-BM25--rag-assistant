Using TransactionProxyFactoryBean is not encouraged in modern Spring applications, although it still works. The typical approach nowadays is to annotate classes with @Transactional , and then stick this element in your application context file: This and other strategies are discussed in great depth in the reference document, and there's even a side note about TransactionProxyFactoryBean .
