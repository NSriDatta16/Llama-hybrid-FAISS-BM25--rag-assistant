To remove non stationarity in a time series, we can standardize the time series by subtracting the mean and dividing by the standard deviation. This is not true. Where did you get this from? Consider this: $y_t=t+\varepsilon_t$, where $\varepsilon_t\sim\mathcal{N}(0,1)$ and $t\in [1..T]$. $$E[y_t]=\sum_ty_t=\frac{(1+T)T}{2T}=\frac{1+T}{2}$$ $$Var[y_t]=Var[t]+1=\frac{T^2+11}{12}$$ Transform the series: $$z_t=\frac{y_t-E[t]}{\sqrt{Var[y_t]}} =\frac{t+\varepsilon_t-\frac{1+T}{2}}{\sqrt{\frac{T^2+11}{12}}} =-\frac{\frac{1+T}{2}}{\sqrt{\frac{T^2+11}{12}}}+\frac{1}{\sqrt{\frac{T^2+11}{12}}}t +\frac{1}{\sqrt{\frac{T^2+11}{12}}}\varepsilon_t$$ The transformed series $z_t$ is still non-stationary series.
