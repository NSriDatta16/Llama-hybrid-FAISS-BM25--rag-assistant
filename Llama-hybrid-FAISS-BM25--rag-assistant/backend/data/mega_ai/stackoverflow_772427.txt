There is absolutely no reason to use the first form - it will be much harder to work with in the Java code, and there will be a lot of bugs resulting from that. All decent O/R mappers (Hibernate being the most popular one) will translate from the DB schema into proper OO object graphs with no problems. However, the downside is that having all this done for you can result in very bad performance when you leave everything to the OR mapper and it fetches too much or too little data from the DB, so you have to pay some attention to caching and whether your collections are lazy or eager loading .
