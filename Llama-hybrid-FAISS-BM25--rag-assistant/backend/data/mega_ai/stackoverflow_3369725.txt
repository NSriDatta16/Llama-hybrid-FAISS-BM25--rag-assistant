I guess the parameters you're passing to Request.QueryString() should be enclosed in quotes: Request.QueryString("payer_email") UPDATE: Yes, and as @PHPology suggested , you should remove the and simply use string concatenation with & . Try it like this: Set rstSimple = cnnSimple.Execute("insert into SALT (Email, Username, FirstName, LastName, ActivationCode, TransactionID, ClientID) VALUES ('" & Request.QueryString("payer_email") & "', '" & Request.QueryString("payer_email") & "', '" & Request.QueryString("first_name") & "', '" & Request.QueryString("last_name") & "', '" & Request.QueryString("hash") & "', '" & Request.QueryString("txn_id") & "', '" & Request.QueryString("client_id") & "')") Although not related to the problem, your query also appears to be vulnerable to SQL Injection .
