Yes. Recent versions of IE (IE8 or above) let you adjust that. Here's how: Fire up Internet Explorer. Click the 'Tools' menu, then click 'Developer Tools'. Alternatively, just press F12 . That should open the Developer Tools window. That window has two menu items that are of interest: Browser Mode. This setting determines the value of the user-agent header sent for every request. Document Mode. This setting determines how the rendering engine renders the page. More at http://blogs.msdn.com/b/ie/archive/2010/06/16/ie-s-compatibility-features-for-site-developers.aspx
