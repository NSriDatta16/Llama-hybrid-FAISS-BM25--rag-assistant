They are not macros, they are just converted by the compiler into the appropriate data storage and operations. For example when you have in your program: int i; i=5 i+=7; The compiler translates it to something similar to this: Allocate sizeof(int) bytes in the stack Put the number 5 in the allocated space Retrieve the number on the allocated space, add 7 to it, and save it again at the same location
