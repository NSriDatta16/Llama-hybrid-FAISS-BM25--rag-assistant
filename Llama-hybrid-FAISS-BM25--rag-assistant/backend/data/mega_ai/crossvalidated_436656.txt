COMPARE THE AVERAGE LENGTH OF THE TWO CONFIDENCE INTERVALS

Question 14 page 399 - Mood Graybill and Boes (1974): In sampling from a normal population compare the average length of the two confidence intervals for the mean $\mu$ . when (a) $\sigma$ is known and (b) $\sigma$ is unknown. I know a) $$IC_\gamma\left(\mu\right)=\left(\bar{X}-z_{\frac{1+\gamma}{2}}\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}},\bar{X}+z_{\frac{1+\gamma}{2}}\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}\right)$$ And b) $$IC_\gamma\left(\mu\right)=\left(\bar{X}-t_{\left(n-1;\frac{1+\gamma}{2}\right)}\frac{S}{\sqrt{n}},\bar{X}+t_{\left(n-1;\frac{1+\gamma}{2}\right)}\frac{S}{\sqrt{n}}\right)$$ $$E\left[l\left(I_1\right) \right]=2z_{\frac{1+\gamma}{2}}\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$$ $$E\left[l\left(I_2\right) \right] =\frac{2t_{\left(n-1;\frac{1+\gamma}{2}\right)}}{\sqrt{n}}\sqrt{\frac{2}{n-1}}\frac{\Gamma\left(\frac{n}{2}\right)}{\Gamma\left(\frac{n-1}{2}\right)}\sigma$$ $$\frac{E\left[l\left(I_2\right) \right]}{E\left[l\left(I_1\right) \right]}=\frac{t_{\left(n-1;\frac{1+\gamma}{2}\right)}}{z_{\frac{1+\gamma}{2}}}\sqrt{\frac{2}{n-1}}\frac{\Gamma\left(\frac{n}{2}\right)}{\Gamma\left(\frac{n-1}{2}\right)}$$ Any idea? How I can prove $\frac{E\left[l\left(I_2\right) \right]}{E\left[l\left(I_1\right) \right]}>1$ or $\frac{E\left[l\left(I_2\right) \right]}{E\left[l\left(I_1\right) \right]} ?
