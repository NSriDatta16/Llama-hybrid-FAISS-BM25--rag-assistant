This is a common cause for surprise. You are calculating the confidence interval of the difference in proportions, not the proportions themselves. So it is perfectly possible for the difference to be negative. The difference has to lie between -1 and 1. Just for completeness in his paper entitled "Interval estimation for the difference between independent proportions: comparison of eleven methods" available here Robert Newcombe gives some better methods which ensure the difference remains legal.
