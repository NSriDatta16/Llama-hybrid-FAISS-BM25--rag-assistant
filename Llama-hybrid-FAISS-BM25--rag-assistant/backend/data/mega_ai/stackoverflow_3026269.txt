Regex's are too fragile for HTML parsing. In your example, the following would strip out your attributes: echo preg_replace( "| /]+)?|", " \n hello \n \n" ); Update Make to second capture optional and do not strip '/' from closing tags: | ]+)| to | /]+)?| Demonstrate this regular expression works: $ phpsh Starting php type 'h' or 'help' to see instructions & features php> $html = ' hello '; php> echo preg_replace("| /]+)?|", " hello php> $html = ' hello '; php> echo preg_replace("| /]+)?|", " hello
