The random variables $Z_i:=\mathbb{1}_{\{X_i\,\in\,B\}}$ for $i\in\{1,\ldots,n\}$ are i.i.d $\text{Bernoulli}$ with unknown parameter $\theta=\mathbb{P}(X\in B).$ Using a $\text{Uniform}(0,1)$ non-informative prior distribution for $\theta$ the posterior distribution is $\text{Beta}(1+nT_n(B),1+n(1-T_n(B))).$ Under a quadratic loss penalization, the bayesian point estimate for $\theta=\mathbb{P}(X\in B)$ is the posterior expected value which is the following: $$\theta^* \,=\, \frac{1+nT_n(B)}{2+n}$$
