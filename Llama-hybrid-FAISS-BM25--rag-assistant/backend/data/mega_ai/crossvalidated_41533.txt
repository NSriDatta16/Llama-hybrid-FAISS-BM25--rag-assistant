In general, absolute goodness of fit measures are not available for models different than linear regression. If you obtain a significant p - value for some predictor this means that the model you are fitting is better that using the null model (no predictor, just the relative frequency of the outcome). This does not imply that you would be able to obtain a good prediction of the outcome. In my personal opinion logistic regression models are good to rank subjects about their relative risk. The Gini index could be used to see how well the risk ranking works. $normalizedGini
