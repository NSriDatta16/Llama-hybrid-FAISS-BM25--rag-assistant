A LEFT OUTER JOIN will do the trick INSERT INTO dashboardtasks1 (tour, taskname, deptdate, tasktype, desc, duedate, compdate, comments, agent, compby, graceperiod) SELECT s.tour, s.taskname, s.deptdate, s.tasktype, s.desc, s.duedate, s.compdate, s.comments, s.agent, s.compby, s.graceperiod FROM staggingtasks s LEFT OUTER JOIN dashboardtasks1 d ON d.tour=s.tour and d.taskname=s.taskname and d.deptdate=s.deptdate WHERE d.tour IS NULL What you are doing here is joining the two tables on the values you want to match. If there isn't a row in dashboardtasks1 where the values you've joined on exist (in this case tour,taskname and deptdate) it will insert a row. Otherwise it won't.
