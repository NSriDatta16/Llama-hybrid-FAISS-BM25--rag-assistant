The Gang of Four Design Patterns book is basically all about why to prefer composition over inheritance and offers many ways to do that. Some reasons: The number of classes increases the complexity of the codebase. In many newer languages, inheritance is limited to one class, while you can compose as much as you want. Base classes cannot be changed at runtime (essentially the issue behind what you are running into).
