(Oracle) how to group rows for pagination

I have a table that outputs similar to this (although in thousands): EMPNO ENAME TRANDATE AMT ---------- ---------- --------- ------- 100 Alison 21-MAR-96 45000 100 Alison 12-DEC-78 23000 100 Alison 24-OCT-82 11000 101 Linda 15-JAN-84 16000 101 Linda 30-JUL-87 17000 102 Celia 31-DEC-90 78000 102 Celia 17-SEP-96 21000 103 James 21-MAR-96 45000 103 James 12-DEC-78 23000 103 James 24-OCT-82 11000 104 Robert 15-JAN-84 16000 104 Robert 30-JUL-87 17000 My desired output would be similar to this: EMPNO ENAME TRANDATE AMT PAGE ---------- ---------- --------- ------- ---- 100 Alison 21-MAR-96 45000 1 100 Alison 12-DEC-78 23000 1 100 Alison 24-OCT-82 11000 1 101 Linda 15-JAN-84 16000 2 101 Linda 30-JUL-87 17000 2 102 Celia 31-DEC-90 78000 2 102 Celia 17-SEP-96 21000 2 103 James 21-MAR-96 45000 3 104 Robert 12-DEC-78 23000 4 104 Robert 24-OCT-82 11000 4 104 Robert 15-JAN-84 16000 4 104 Robert 30-JUL-87 17000 4 Basically, it should insert a new field to identify the page it belongs to. The page break is based on the rows. And, as if "kept together" in EMPNO, it adds 1 to PAGE when the rows cannot add the next EMPNO batch. It's for the Excel limit since Excel does not allow more than 65000 rows (or so) in a single Sheet. In the sample's case, it's only 4 rows. The limit number is static.
