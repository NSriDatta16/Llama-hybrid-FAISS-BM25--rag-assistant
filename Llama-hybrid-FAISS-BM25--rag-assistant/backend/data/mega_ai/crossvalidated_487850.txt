One way to show this is by looking at the empirical CDF (ECDF) plots for the two groups. The ECDFs use data to imitate the respective population CDFs. The ECDF for FALSE is in blue. Generally, the dominating plot is below and to the right of the other one (requiring higher values to ascend from 0 at left to 1 at right). [Here, the dominating segments are below the others because both groups take only values 2, 3, 4, and 5.] plot(ecdf(value[group==T]), col="brown", main="ECDFs of FALSE (blue) and TRUE Groups") lines(ecdf(value[group==F]), col="blue") Often when one has to look at ECDF plots to understand the meaning of a Wilcoxon signed rank test, it is difficult to explain to non-statisticians what kind of difference between groups the test has found.
