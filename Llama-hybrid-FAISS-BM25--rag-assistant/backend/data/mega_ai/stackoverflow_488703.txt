I haven't read or listened to Joel's comments so can't comment on those specifically. I think you have to look at the single responsibility principle in terms of a goal, rather than a strict requirement. As with many things in software development, there are ideals that should be strived for but, unless your code has no monetary benefit or cost, you have to be pragmatic in serving your customer's needs.
