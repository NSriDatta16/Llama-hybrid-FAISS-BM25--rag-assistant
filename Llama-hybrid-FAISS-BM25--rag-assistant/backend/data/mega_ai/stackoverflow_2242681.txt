Forgetting the contrived example, everything I've read about iPhone dev best practices says that autorelease pools are discouraged Autorelease pools are not discouraged; they're used everywhere in Cocoa, and when used correctly they have zero impact on memory usage. Autorelease pools are only a concern when an they artificially extend the life of a large number of objects, say in a loop, in which case you would manage your autorelease pools manually.
