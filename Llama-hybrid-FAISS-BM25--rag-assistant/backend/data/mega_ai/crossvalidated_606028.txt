When the AR(2) process $(1 - \phi_1B - \phi_2B^2)X_t = Z_t$ is causal $^\dagger$ , Example 3.3.5 in Time Series: Theory and Methods by P. J. Brockwell and R. A. Davis gives the general term formula of $\gamma(h)$ as follows: \begin{align} \gamma(h) = \frac{\sigma^2\xi_1^2\xi_2^2}{(\xi_1\xi_2 - 1)(\xi_2 - \xi_1)}[(\xi_1^2 - 1)^{-1}\xi_1^{1 - h} - (\xi_2^2 - 1)^{-1}\xi_2^{1 - h}], \tag{1} \end{align} where $\xi_1, \xi_2$ are the zeros of the quadratic equation $1 - \phi_1z - \phi_2z^2 = 0$ . $^\dagger$ Causality requires that in $(1)$ , $|\xi_1| > 1, |\xi_2| > 1$ . $(1)$ only treats the case that $\xi_1 \neq \xi_2$ . The case $\xi_1 = \xi_2$ is covered in Example 3.3.4, where the general term formula for $\gamma(h)$ of the causal AR( $p$ ) process is given as: \begin{align} \gamma(h) = \sum_{i = 1}^k\sum_{j = 0}^{r_i - 1}\beta_{ij}h^j\xi_i^{-h}, \tag{2} \end{align} where $\xi_i, i = 1, \ldots, k$ , are the zeros (possibly complex) of $\phi(z)$ , and $r_i$ is the multiplicity of $\xi_i$ , the constants $\beta_{ij}$ are determined by boundary conditions: \begin{align} & \gamma(0) - \phi_1\gamma(- 1) - \cdots - \phi_p\gamma(- p) = \sigma^2, \\ & \gamma(k) - \phi_1\gamma(k - 1) - \cdots - \phi_p\gamma(k - p) = 0, k \geq 1. \tag{3} \end{align} When $p = 2, k = 1, r_1 = 2$ , we have $\phi_2 = -\frac{1}{4}\phi_1^2$ , $(2)$ becomes \begin{align} \gamma(h) = \beta_{10} + \beta_{11}h\xi. \tag{4} \end{align} and $(3)$ becomes: \begin{align} & \gamma(0) - \phi_1\gamma(1) - \phi_2\gamma_2(2) = \sigma^2, \\ & \gamma(1) - \phi_1\gamma(0) - \phi_2\gamma_2(1) = 0, \\ & \gamma(2) - \phi_1\gamma(1) - \phi_2\gamma_2(0) = 0. \tag{5} \end{align} Solving $(5)$ yields \begin{align} & \gamma(0) = \frac{64 + 16\phi_1^2}{64 - 48\phi_1^2 + 12\phi_1^4 - \phi_1^6}\sigma^2, \\ & \gamma(1) = \frac{64\phi_1}{64 - 48\phi_1^2 + 12\phi_1^4 - \phi_1^6}\sigma^2, \\ & \gamma(2) = \frac{48\phi_1^2 - 4\phi_1^4}{64 - 48\phi_1^2 + 12\phi_1^4 - \phi_1^6}\sigma^2. \end{align} It then follows by $(4)$ and $\phi_1 = 2\xi^{-1}$ that \begin{align} & \beta_{10} = \gamma(0) = \frac{1 + \xi^{-2}}{ï¼ˆ1 - \xi^{-2})^3}\sigma^2, \\ & \beta_{11} = (\gamma(1) - \beta_{10})\xi^{-1} =-\frac{\xi^{-1}(1 - \xi^{-1})^2}{(1 - \xi^{-2})^3}\sigma^2. \end{align} This shows that the autocovariance function of the AR( $2$ ) process $(1 - \xi^{-1}B)^2X_t = Z_t$ is \begin{align} \gamma(h) = \frac{1 + \xi^{-2}}{(1 - \xi^{-2})^3} - \frac{(1 - \xi^{-1})^2}{(1 - \xi^{-2})^3}h. \end{align}
