MAP@k is normally used in recommendation systems, but also in other kinds of systems. Quoting from here : If you have an algorithm that is returning a ranked ordering of items, each item is either hit or miss (like relevant vs. irrelevant search results) and items further down in the list are less likely to be used (like search results at the bottom of the page), then maybe MAP is the metric for you! Some application examples are these Kaggle competitions: Expedia Hotel Recommendations Santander Product Recommendation National Data Science Challenge 2019 And these are some resources with more information about it: Mean Average Precision (MAP) For Recommender Systems How mean Average Precision at k (mAP@k) can be more useful than other evaluation metrics
