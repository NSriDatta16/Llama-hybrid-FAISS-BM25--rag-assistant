First, acknowledge that he's right: copy-paste is indeed faster now . Then, say that the problem is the cost in the long run, and that the cost will increase because with duplication, the system isn't as well-ordered as it could be. He's introducing disorder, clutter, and the more disorder you have, the harder it is to work with a system. Applying some effort now to organize it better will (usually) pay off in the long term. It's just like keeping your desk or room organized. This is Ivar Jakobson's idea of software entropy
