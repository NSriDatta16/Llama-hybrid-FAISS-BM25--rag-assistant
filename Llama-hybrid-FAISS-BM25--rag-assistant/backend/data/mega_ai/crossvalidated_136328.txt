t -test assumes that your data is normally distributed, what is simply not true about proportions - you should rather assume distributions such as Binomial . There are statistical tests that are especially designed for proportions data like Proportions test ( prop.test in R) or Binomial test ( binom.test in R) (see here , here , or here ). In medical research odds ratios and logistic regression are commonly used for proportions. So there are multiple tools to choose from. Take also look at this thread .
