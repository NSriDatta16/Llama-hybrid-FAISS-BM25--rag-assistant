The points move away from the origin. We rewrite the dynamic of the point as follows $$ \begin{pmatrix} x_{n+1} \\ y_{n+1} \\ \end{pmatrix} =\begin{pmatrix} x_{n} \\ y_{n} \\ \end{pmatrix} + \sqrt{x_n^2 + y_n^2} \cdot \mathcal{N}_2 \left(\begin{pmatrix} 0\\ 0\\ \end{pmatrix};\begin{pmatrix} 1 &0 \\ 0&1 \\ \end{pmatrix} \right) =\begin{pmatrix} x_{n} \\ y_{n} \\ \end{pmatrix} + \sqrt{\frac{x_n^2 + y_n^2}{2}} \cdot \begin{pmatrix} u_{n} \\ v_{n} \\ \end{pmatrix} $$ where the vectors $(u_n,v_n)'$ are iid and follow the bi-dimensional standard normal distribution. These $(u_n, v_n)_n$ are also independent to $(x_n,y_n)_n$ . Let's denote $r_n = \sqrt{x_n^2 + y_n^2}$ be the distance of the point $n$ -th and the origin. We have $$\begin{align} r_{n+1}^2 &= x_{n+1}^2 + y_{n+1}^2 \\ &= \left(x_n + \frac{r_n}{\sqrt{2}}u_n\right)^2 + \left(y_n + \frac{r_n}{\sqrt{2}}v_n\right)^2\\ &= r_n^2 \left(1+ \sqrt{2}\left(\frac{x_n}{r_n}u_n +\frac{y_n}{r_n}v_n \right) + \frac{u_n^2 + v_n^2}{2}\right) \end{align}$$ We deduce that $$\frac{r_{n+1}^2}{r_n^2} = 1+ \sqrt{2}\left(\frac{x_n}{r_n}u_n +\frac{y_n}{r_n}v_n \right) + \frac{u_n^2 + v_n^2}{2} \tag{1}$$ From $(1)$ , we have: $$\begin{align} r_{n+1} \le r_n &\iff \sqrt{2}\left(\frac{x_n}{r_n}u_n +\frac{y_n}{r_n}v_n \right) + \frac{u_n^2 + v_n^2}{2} \le 0 \\ &\iff \left(u_n +\sqrt{2}\frac{x_n}{r_n} \right)^2+\left(v_n +\sqrt{2}\frac{y_n}{r_n} \right)^2 \le 2 \tag{2} \end{align}$$ From $(2)$ , we deduce that $$\begin{align} \mathbb{P}(r_{n+1} \le r_n) &= \mathbb{P}\left( \left(u_n +\sqrt{2}\frac{x_n}{r_n} \right)^2+\left(v_n +\sqrt{2}\frac{y_n}{r_n} \right)^2 \le 2 \right) \\ &= \mathbb{P} (u_n^2 +(v_n-\sqrt{2})^2 \le 2) \tag{3} \end{align}$$ Indeed, the last equality of $(3)$ is because, geographically, the probability is independently the position of the point $\left(\frac{x_n}{r_n}, \frac{y_n}{r_n}\right)$ lying on the circle of radius $\sqrt{2}$ centered at the origin $O$ . Using Mathematica, NProbability[ u^2 + (v - Sqrt[2])^2 we obtain that $$\mathbb{P} (u_n^2 +(v_n-1)^2 \le 2) \approx 0.345746 \tag{4}$$ By consequence, from $(3)$ and $(4)$ : $$\color{red}{\mathbb{P}(r_{n+1} \le r_n) \approx 0.345746 However, can we conclude that the points move away from the origin because the probability that $r_{n+1} \le r_n$ is smaller (less that $0.5$ )? No, we can't. Even if the probability that $r_{n+1} \le r_n$ is smaller, it is possible that whenever $r_{n+1} \le r_n$ , the ratio $\frac{r_{n+1}}{r_n}$ may be very small, and then in average, the points come close to the origin. We need to compute then the expectation of $\frac{r_{n+1}^2}{r_n}$ in case of $r_{n+1} \le r_n$ and in case of $r_{n+1} \ge r_n$ to quantify the movement of the point $n+1$ -th in function of the point $n$ -th. First, we compute the expectation of $\frac{r_{n+1}^2}{r_n} \mathbf{1}_{\{r_{n+1}\le r_n\}}$ , from $(1)$ and $(2)$ we have: $$\begin{align} \mathbb{E}\left(\frac{r_{n+1}^2}{r_n^2} \mathbf{1}_{\{r_{n+1}\le r_n\}} \right) &= \frac{1}{2}\mathbb{E}\left(\left(\left(u_n +\sqrt{2}\frac{x_n}{r_n} \right)^2+\left(v_n +\sqrt{2}\frac{y_n}{r_n} \right)^2\right) \mathbf{1}_{\{\left(u_n +\sqrt{2}\frac{x_n}{r_n} \right)^2+\left(v_n +\sqrt{2}\frac{y_n}{r_n} \right)^2 \le 2\}} \right) \end{align}$$ By the same argument as in $(3)$ , by rotating the plan $Oxy$ , we have: $$\mathbb{E}\left(\frac{r_{n+1}^2}{r_n^2} \mathbf{1}_{\{r_{n+1}\le r_n\}} \right) = \frac{1}{2}\mathbb{E} \left((u_n^2 +(v_n-\sqrt{2})^2)\mathbf{1}_{\{u_n^2 +(v_n-\sqrt{2})^2 \le 2\}}\right)$$ Using Mathematica, NExpectation[ 0.5*(u^2 + (v - Sqrt[2])^2)* Boole[u^2 + (v - Sqrt[2])^2 We have $$\mathbb{E}\left(\frac{r_{n+1}^2}{r_n^2} \mathbf{1}_{\{r_{n+1}\le r_n\}} \right) \approx 0.167714 \tag{5}$$ By similar method, we prove also that $$\mathbb{E}\left(\frac{r_{n+1}^2}{r_n^2} \mathbf{1}_{\{r_{n+1}\ge r_n\}} \right) \approx 1.83229 \tag{6}$$ From $(5)$ , $(6)$ and $(3)$ , by using the definition of conditional expectation, we deduce that $$\color{red}{\mathbb{E}\left(\frac{r_{n+1}^2}{r_n^2} | r_{n+1}\le r_n \right)} =\frac{\mathbb{E}\left(\frac{r_{n+1}^2}{r_n^2} \mathbf{1}_{\{r_{n+1}\le r_n\}} \right)}{\mathbb{P}(r_{n+1} \le r_n)} \approx \frac{0.167714}{0.345746} \color{red}{\approx 0.485079} \tag{7}$$ $$\color{red}{\mathbb{E}\left(\frac{r_{n+1}^2}{r_n^2} | r_{n+1}\ge r_n \right)} =\frac{\mathbb{E}\left(\frac{r_{n+1}^2}{r_n^2} \mathbf{1}_{\{r_{n+1}\ge r_n\}} \right)}{\mathbb{P}(r_{n+1} \ge r_n)} \approx \frac{1.83229}{1-0.345746} \color{red}{\approx 2.80057} \tag{8}$$ From $(3), (7), (8)$ , we observe that: Not only : the probability of the event $\text{reduced} := \{r_{n+1} \le r_n\}$ is smaller than the probability of the event $\text{increased} := \{r_{n+1} \ge r_n\}$ But also: when the event $\text{reduced}$ is realized, the expectation of $\frac{r_{n+1}^2}{r_n^2}$ is only reduced by $0.485079$ (around half) while the expectation when the event $\text{increased}$ is realized, the increase is $2.80057$ (nearly three times). We can conclude that for each iteration, the points have a tendency to move away from the origin $O$ .
