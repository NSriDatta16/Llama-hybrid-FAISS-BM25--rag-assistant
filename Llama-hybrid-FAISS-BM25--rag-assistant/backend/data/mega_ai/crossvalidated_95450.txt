$B$ is not necessary positive definite unless all averages are positive. Your expression can be simplified: $$\frac{1}{I}\sum_{i=1}^I \mu^i B {\mu^{i}}'- \bar\mu^i B {\bar\mu^{i}}'=\frac{1}{I}\sum_{i=1}^I \sum_{k=1}^K \frac{(\mu_k^i)^2}{\bar\mu_k} - \sum_{k=1}^K \bar\mu_k=$$ $$=\sum_{k=1}^K \frac{1}{\bar\mu_k} [\frac{1}{I}\sum_{i=1}^I (\mu_k^i)^2 - \bar\mu_k^2]=\sum_{k=1}^K \frac{Var(\mu_k^i)}{\bar\mu_k}=s'Bs$$ where $s=\sqrt{diag(Var(\mu^i))}: K \times 1$ vector of standard deviations. However, this expression is undefined if any $\bar\mu_k=0$.
