If I understand correctly, you're trying to see what item is being dropped on. What you need is the ItemAtPos function of the ListBox. It takes a TPoint parameter and the OnDragDrop event handler has the X and Y coordinates. In this example, ListBox2 is the Source, and ListBox1 is the control being dropped onto. iItem gives me the ItemIndex of the ListBox1 item being dropped onto. procedure TForm1.ListBox1DragDrop(Sender, Source: TObject; X, Y: Integer); var iItem: Integer; MyPoint: TPoint; begin MyPoint.X := X; MyPoint.Y := Y; iItem := ListBox1.ItemAtPos(MyPoint, True); ListBox1.Items.Insert(iItem, ListBox2.Items[ListBox2.ItemIndex]); end; There is no range checking here, it's just an example to illustrate the ItemAtPos function.
