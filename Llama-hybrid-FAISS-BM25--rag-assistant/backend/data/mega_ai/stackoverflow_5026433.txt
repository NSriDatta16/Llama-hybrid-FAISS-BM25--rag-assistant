Note: The following assumes you're using Jetty 7.2.2+ (probably works with any Jetty 7.0+). The problem is you're missing ONE extra layer of indirection. Even though you've configured Jetty in your webapp, you still need to tell the Jetty container that it needs to look for jetty-env.xml in your webapp. To do that, add the following to your jetty.xml (under ${JETTY_INSTALL_DIR}/etc: org.eclipse.jetty.webapp.configuration org.eclipse.jetty.webapp.WebInfConfiguration org.eclipse.jetty.webapp.WebXmlConfiguration org.eclipse.jetty.webapp.MetaInfConfiguration org.eclipse.jetty.webapp.FragmentConfiguration org.eclipse.jetty.plus.webapp.EnvConfiguration org.eclipse.jetty.plus.webapp.PlusConfiguration org.eclipse.jetty.webapp.JettyWebXmlConfiguration org.eclipse.jetty.webapp.TagLibConfiguration NOW Jetty will know to parse the jetty-env.xml you've created.
