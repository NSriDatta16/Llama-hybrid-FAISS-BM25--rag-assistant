For question 1, the estimate of the 49.7. An estimate is a number. The standard error of that estimate is a measure of how good the estimate is, given certain assumptions. I'm not sure I understand your question 2, but I do know that the concept of confidence interval is very confusing and one must be careful. The definition given in the text that you quote is correct. Many people get this confused with the notion of a credible interval that comes from Bayesian statistics.
