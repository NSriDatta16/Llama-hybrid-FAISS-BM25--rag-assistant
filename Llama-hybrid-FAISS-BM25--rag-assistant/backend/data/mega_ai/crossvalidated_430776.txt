It depends on what language are you working on and on what task are you trying to solve Lemmatization would more likely help in topic modeling task , as suggested on: https://opendatagroup.github.io/data%20science/2019/03/21/preprocessing-text.html Topic modeling, for example, relies on the distribution of content words, the identification of which is dependent on a string match between words, which is achieved by lemmatizing their forms so that all variants are consistent across documents Lemmatization would more likely result in less accuracy in sentiment analysis , as suggested on: https://www.quora.com/Is-it-normal-to-get-better-accuracy-without-stemming-and-lemmatization-than-using-them-in-NLP-text-classification For example, past tensed words could be judged as more negative / more likely to occur in negative sentences than present tense. The word hated could be more negative than hates .
