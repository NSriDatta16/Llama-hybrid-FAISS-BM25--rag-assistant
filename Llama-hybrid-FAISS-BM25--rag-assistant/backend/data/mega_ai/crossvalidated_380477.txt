"Is it simply a matter of calculating what the delay is e.g. by analysing when peak radiation is in comparison to peak cooling and adjusting the radiation data by this time factor? " Essentially Yes ! But it appears that you are locking in the temporal response. With time series effects you often have dynamic structure where particular input can have an effect on Y for more than 1 period. Also be aware that there may be anomalous data points that require special handling. You might want to read http://www.autobox.com/pdfs/regvsbox-old.pdf and http://docplayer.net/12080848-Outliers-level-shifts-and-variance-changes-in-time-series.html and http://faculty.chicagobooth.edu/ruey.tsay/teaching/uts/lec10-08.pdf as time series brings special opportunities.
