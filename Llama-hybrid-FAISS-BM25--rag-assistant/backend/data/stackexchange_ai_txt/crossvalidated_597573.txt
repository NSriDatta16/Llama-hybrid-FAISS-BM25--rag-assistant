System-level and segment-level correlation

In a few NLP papers such as Re-evaluating Evaluation in Text Summarization (Bhandari et al., EMNLP 2020), two types of correlation strategies are presented. I had a discussion with colleagues over the rationale behind those correlation types and we were unable to arrive at a consensus. Setup : For each document $d_i$ , $i \in \{1, \dots, N\}$ in a dataset, we have $J$ system outputs. Let $y_i^j$ , $j \in \{1, \dots, J\}$ be the $j$ -th output of the $i$ -th document, $m_1$ and $m_2$ be two evaluation metrics and $K$ a correlation measure (such as Pearson's $r$ ). The segment-level correlation is defined as: $$K^\textrm{segment}_{m_1, m_2} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^N K \left( \begin{bmatrix} m_1(y_{i}^1) \\ \vdots \\ m_1(y_{i}^J) \\ \end{bmatrix} , \begin{bmatrix} m_2(y_{i}^1) \\ \vdots \\ m_2(y_{i}^J) \\ \end{bmatrix} \right) $$ and the system-level correlation as: $$K^\textrm{system}_{m_1, m_2} = K \left( \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^N \begin{bmatrix} m_1(y_{i}^1) \\ \vdots \\ m_1(y_{i}^J) \\ \end{bmatrix} , \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^N \begin{bmatrix} m_2(y_{i}^1) \\ \vdots \\ m_2(y_{i}^J) \\ \end{bmatrix} \right) $$ In particular, we were unsure about the segment-level correlation: why do we average over correlations instead of concatenating all vectors for all documents such as: $$K^\textrm{segment}_{m_1, m_2} = K \left( \begin{bmatrix} m_1(y_{1}^1) \\ \vdots \\ m_1(y_{1}^J) \\ m_1(y_{2}^1) \\ \vdots \\ m_1(y_{2}^J) \\ \vdots \\ m_1(y_{n}^1) \\ \vdots \\ m_1(y_{n}^J) \\ \end{bmatrix} , \begin{bmatrix} m_2(y_{1}^1) \\ \vdots \\ m_2(y_{1}^J) \\ m_2(y_{2}^1) \\ \vdots \\ m_2(y_{2}^J) \\ \vdots \\ m_2(y_{n}^1) \\ \vdots \\ m_2(y_{n}^J) \\ \end{bmatrix} \right) $$ Or why don't we average over the $J$ axis rather than the $n$ axis such as: $$K^\textrm{system}_{m_1, m_2} = K \left( \frac{1}{J} \sum_{j=1}^J \begin{bmatrix} m_1(y_{1}^j) \\ \vdots \\ m_1(y_{N}^j) \\ \end{bmatrix} , \frac{1}{J} \sum_{j=1}^J \begin{bmatrix} m_2(y_{1}^j) \\ \vdots \\ m_2(y_{N}^j) \\ \end{bmatrix} \right) $$ Finally, what could be the practical significance and usage of those correlation types?
