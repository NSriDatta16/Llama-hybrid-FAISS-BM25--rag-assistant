If you can permit an exception to be thrown on bad data, the second approach is best. This is common in situations where the administrators have control of the quality of the data. However, it's often true that the administrators can't guarantee the quality of the data. In these cases, it's frequently a requirement that the application handle malformed data gracefully. When faced with this need, the first approach is an elegant way to do that.
