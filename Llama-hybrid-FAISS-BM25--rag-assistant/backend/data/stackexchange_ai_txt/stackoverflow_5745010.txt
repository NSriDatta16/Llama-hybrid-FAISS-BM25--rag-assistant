"Thousands of rows per day" should be pocket change for Sql Server. First, I voted up Alex K answer. File groups will get you to where you want to be most likely. Partitioned tables may be overkill, and is only available in Enterprise version and is not for the light hearted. What I will add is: http://www.google.com/#q=glenn+berry+dmv&bav=on.2,or.r_gc.r_pw.&fp=73d2ceaabb6b01bf&hl=en You need to tweak your indexes. In the good vs. better vs. best category, Glenn Berry's DMV queries are "better". Those queries will help you fix the majority of issues. In the "best" category is pain staking looking at each stored procedure, and looking at the execution plan and trying out different things. This is what a good dba is able to provide.
