That's probably among the worst practices you can come across in php code. It basically tells the interpreter to suppress errors and just try to do whatever the code asks it to do regardless of the outcome. A great way to drag yourself and fellow teammates into all-nighter phantom bug hunts once the app has grown substantially. Try-catch with custom exception handling is the way to go.
