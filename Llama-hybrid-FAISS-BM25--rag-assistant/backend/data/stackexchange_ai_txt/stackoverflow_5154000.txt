Found it: It is ~:* It tells lisp to reuse the last argument. Like rewinding the arguments one place back. For the whole explanation paragraph see: http://www.gigamonkeys.com/book/a-few-format-recipes.html (it is near the bottom of the page) So it becomes (format nil "One occurence of ~C , another one: ~:*~C , and yet another one: ~:*~C" #\a) => "One occurence of a , another one: a , and yet another one: a"
