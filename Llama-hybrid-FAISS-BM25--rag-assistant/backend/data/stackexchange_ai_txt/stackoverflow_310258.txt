respondsToSelector: almost certainly is better than you maintaining a table of what given releases do and do not implement. Be lazy. Let the runtime tell you whether it can do something or not, and fall back to older methods when you need to. Your code will be far less fragile because you don't have to maintain your own global data that the rest of your code has to keep checking with.
