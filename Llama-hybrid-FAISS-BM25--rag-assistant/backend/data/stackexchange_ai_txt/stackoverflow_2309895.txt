I guess, that's what POST is for: The 'U' in 'CRUD'. You POST data to an existing resource. The resource decides what to do with it, and gets updated. Also, the POST data may be only a fragment of the complete resource. Twitter's approach is IMHO not RESTful, because they overload GET.
