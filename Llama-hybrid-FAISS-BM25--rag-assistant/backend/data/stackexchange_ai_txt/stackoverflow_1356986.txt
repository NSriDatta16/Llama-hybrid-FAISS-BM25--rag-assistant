File system performance testing is a very complex topic. You can easily make a lots of mistakes that basically make your whole tests worthless. Stony Brook University and IBM Watson Labs have published an highly recommended journal paper in the "Transaction of Storage" about file system benchmarking, in which they present different benchmarks and their strong and weak points: A nine year study of file system and storage benchmarking . They give lots of advise how to design and implement a good filesystem benchmark. As I said: It is not an easy task.
