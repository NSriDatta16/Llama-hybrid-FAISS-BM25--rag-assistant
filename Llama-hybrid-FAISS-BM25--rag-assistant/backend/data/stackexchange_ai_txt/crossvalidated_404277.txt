There is a nice posting that contains this already on Stack Exchange. Credible intervals are not confidence intervals. Read What's the difference between a confidence interval and a credible interval? . Bayesian credible intervals can have better or worse coverage properties than confidence intervals. Good priors are equivalent to adding to the sample, which makes the intervals narrower and improves coverage.
