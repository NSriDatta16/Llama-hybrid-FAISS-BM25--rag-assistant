Yes. You never know when a promised service will surprise by not working. Best to report an error even for the surprises. Otherwise you will find yourself with a customer saying your application doesn't work, and the reason will be a complete mystery; you won't be able to respond in a timely, useful way to your customer and you both lose. If you organize your code to always do such checks, it isn't that hard to add the next check to that next API you call.
