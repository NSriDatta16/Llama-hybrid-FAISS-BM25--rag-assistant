Bayesian $\chi^2$ discrepancy

We define the $\chi^2$ discrepancy as $$T(y,\theta)=\sum_i\frac{(y_i-E(y_i|\theta))^2}{\mathrm{var}(y_i|\theta)}$$ (definition from Gelman). How do we apply this if $\theta$ is a random variable, and we have samples of it(but not the distribution)?
