40 nanosecond difference in object creation time is background noise in a Intel Core i7 920. Assuming the numbers are an average over several runs, 40 nanoseconds is just 0.04 microseconds. Assuming on Windows 7 64-bit that the High Performance clock was functioning correctly, you're probably looking at hiccups in windows, the phase of the moon, statistical error, measuring program error, memory allocation implementation speeds, or something else entirely.
