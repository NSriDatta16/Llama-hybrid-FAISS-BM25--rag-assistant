It's been optimized away by the compiler, simply because having an instance of the class is not necessary. The compiler basically replaces csm.method(); by CallingStaticMethod.method(); It's in general also a good practice to do so yourself. Even the average IDE would warn you about accessing static methods through an instance, at least Eclipse does here.
