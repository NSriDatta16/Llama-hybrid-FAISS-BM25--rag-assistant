The MSDN documentation for datetime recommends using datetime2 . Here is their recommendation: Use the time , date , datetime2 and datetimeoffset data types for new work. These types align with the SQL Standard. They are more portable. time , datetime2 and datetimeoffset provide more seconds precision. datetimeoffset provides time zone support for globally deployed applications. datetime2 has larger date range, a larger default fractional precision, and optional user-specified precision. Also depending on the user-specified precision it may use less storage.
