No, Perl 5 defines the modulo operator in perlop and even has tests to ensure it works as documented. from perl/t/op/arith.t tryeq $T++, 13 % 4, 1; tryeq $T++, -13 % 4, 3; tryeq $T++, 13 % -4, -3; tryeq $T++, -13 % -4, -1; However, if you use the integer pragma, you are at the mercies of the C compiler.
