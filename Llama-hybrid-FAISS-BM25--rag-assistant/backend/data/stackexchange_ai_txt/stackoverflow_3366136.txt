You can use top in batch mode with 1 (or more) iterations. Then use grep to parse through it. $ top -b -n 1 | grep -2 "load average" top - 12:08:31 up 14 days, 19:03, 26 users, load average: 0.25, 0.45, 0.37 Tasks: 219 total, 1 running, 217 sleeping, 0 stopped, 1 zombie Cpu(s): 4.2%us, 0.6%sy, 0.0%ni, 94.0%id, 1.0%wa, 0.0%hi, 0.3%si, 0.0%st or $ top -b -n 1 | grep "Cpu(s)\:" Cpu(s): 4.2%us, 0.6%sy, 0.0%ni, 94.0%id, 1.0%wa, 0.0%hi, 0.3%si, 0.0%st Further, you use can use awk to get a specific column and work from there. $ top -b -n 1 | grep "Cpu(s)\:" | awk '{print $2}' 4.2%us,
