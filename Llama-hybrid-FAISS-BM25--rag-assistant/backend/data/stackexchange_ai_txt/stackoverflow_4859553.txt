One of five maxims under the Unix Philosophy , as presented by Rob Pike , is this: Data dominates. If you have chosen the right data structures and organized things well, the algorithms will almost always be self-evident. Data structures, not algorithms, are central to programming. It is often shortened to, "write stupid code that uses smart data."
