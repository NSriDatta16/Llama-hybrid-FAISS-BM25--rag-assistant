The clauses here are applied from the first query set up in the union() method, so just reverse where you're putting them: $query1 = DB::select('p.name') ->from(array('person', 'p')) ->where('p.organization', 'LIKE', 'foo%') ->group_by('name') ->order_by('name') ->limit(10); $names = DB::select('sh.name') ->union($query1, FALSE) ->from(array('stakeholder', 'sh')) ->where('sh.organization', 'LIKE', 'foo%') ->execute() ->as_array(); You can also remove that superfluous ->limit(10) from $names since it will be ignored and superseded by the one in $query1 .
