For prototyping proof of concept apps it can sometimes be useful to use programming languages that are well suited to Rapid Application Development. Python is a good example. You have a huge framework to leverage and create the various layers of abstraction you mentioned in your post while still keeping the proof of concept apps code size to a minimum. Working applications speak louder than words so if you could whip up a quick prototype for your team to see and play with it helps make the tumblers fall into place for them.
