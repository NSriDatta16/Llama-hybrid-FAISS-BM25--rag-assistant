Does it matter what it costs? Really? The true answer is "it depends". It depends on loads of things What else the OS is doing at the time How fragmented memory has become speed of the memory and processor on the client PC etc If this code is massively performance critical, them time everything you can and work out the best pattern for your usage case. If it is isn't the most performance critical bit of code, just do whatever is the clearest and simplest to implement and maintain . "We should forget about small efficiencies, say about 97% of the time: premature optimization is the root of all evil", Donald Knuth
