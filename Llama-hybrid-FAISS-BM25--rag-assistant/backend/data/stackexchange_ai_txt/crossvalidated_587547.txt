The paragraph just below the equations provides the proof. Let $D_{m}=\left[e_{\sum_{\ell=1}^{m-1}L_{\ell}+1},e_{\sum_{\ell=1}^{m-1}L_{\ell}+1},\ldots,e_{\sum_{\ell=1}^{m}L_{\ell}}\right]$ $D_{h}=\left[e_{\sum_{\ell=1}^{h-1}L_{\ell}+1},e_{\sum_{\ell=1}^{h-1}L_{\ell}+1},\ldots,e_{\sum_{\ell=1}^{h}L_{\ell}}\right]$ where $e_{j}$ is dim- $\sum_{m=1}^ML_m$ vector where the $j$ th element is 1 and all other elements are 0. Then we have $z_{im}=D_{m}^{\prime}x_{i}$ and $Z_{ih}=D_{h}^{\prime}x_{i}$ Thus the estimator for $\delta$ is $\left(\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}z_{im}x_{i}^{\prime}\right)\left(\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_{i}x_{i}^{\prime}\right)^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_{i}z_{ih}^{\prime}\right)$ = $\left(\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}D_{m}^{\prime}x_{i}x_{i}^{\prime}\right)\left(\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_{i}x_{i}^{\prime}\right)^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_{i}x_{i}^{\prime}D_{h}\right)$ = $D_{m}^{\prime}\left(\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_{i}x_{i}^{\prime}\right)\left(\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_{i}x_{i}^{\prime}\right)^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_{i}x_{i}^{\prime}\right)D_{h}$ = $D_{m}^{\prime}\left(\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_{i}x_{i}^{\prime}\right)D_{h}$ = $\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}\left(D_{m}^{\prime}x_{i}\right)x_{i}^{\prime}D_{h}$ = $\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}\left(D_{m}^{\prime}x_{i}\right)\left(D_{h}^{\prime}x_{i}\right)^{\prime}$ = $\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}z_{im}z_{ih}^{\prime}$
