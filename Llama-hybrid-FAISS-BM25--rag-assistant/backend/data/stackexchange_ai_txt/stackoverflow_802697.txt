in Perl, embedding $$ into a regular expression will result in the PID of the perl process being inserted # Suppose perl has a PID of 5432 my $str1 = "some"; my $str2 = "5432"; print "1 match: $str1" if $str1 =~ /^$$/; print "2 match: $str2" if $str2 =~ /^$$/; Output: 2 match: 5432 Inserting a single $ will match the end of line.
