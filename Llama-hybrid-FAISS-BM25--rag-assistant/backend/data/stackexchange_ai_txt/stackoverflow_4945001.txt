No. ASP.NET can potentially switch threads while processing a request. This is known as thread-agility. There are only certain points where it can/will do this. I can't remember what they are off the top of my head - searching for a reference now... But the short answer is NO : you can't rely on the same thread-local storage being accessible for the entire duration of the request.
