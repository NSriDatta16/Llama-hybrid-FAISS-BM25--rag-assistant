It would be a homogenous polynomial kernel. The constant is usually a hyperparameter that can be changed in most SVM implementations. When it is present (non-zero) the kernel is inhomogenous. What this constant should be will likely depend on your data. For more details, see https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Support_vector_machine#Nonlinear_classification .
