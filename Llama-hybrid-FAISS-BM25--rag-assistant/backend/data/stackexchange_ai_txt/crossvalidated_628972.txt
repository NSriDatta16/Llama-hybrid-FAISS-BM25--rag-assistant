Let $X_1,\ \cdots, X_n$ be i.i.d random variables from $N(\mu,\ 1)$ (By a linear transformation we may assume $\sigma=1$ .). $T=\sum_{i=1}^n X_i.$ Then we claim the conditional density function shall be \begin{align*} &p\left(X=x_1,\ \cdots,\ X_{n-1}=x_{n-1}\mid T=t\right) =\\ &\frac{(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}})^ne^{-\frac{\sum_{i=1}^n (x_i-\mu)^2}{2}-\frac{(t-\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}x_i-\mu)^2}{2}}}{p_T(t)} \tag{$\star$} \label{claim-of-conditional-distribution}\end{align*} , where $p_T$ is the density function of $N(n\mu,\ n)$ . An intuitive explaination is as such: $T\sim N(n\mu,\ n)$ (well-known), and $X_1=x_1,\cdots, X_{n-1}=x_{n-1}, T=t\iff X_1=x_1,\cdots, X_{n-1}=x_{n-1}, X_n=t-\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}x_i$ .
