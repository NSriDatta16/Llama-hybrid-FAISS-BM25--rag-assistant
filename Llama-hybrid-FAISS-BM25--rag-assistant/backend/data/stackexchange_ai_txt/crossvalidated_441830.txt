How to prove P(v|θ')=∫P(v|θ,θ') P(θ|θ')dθ

Given in textbook Bayesian Reasoning and Machine Learning by David Barber in chapter 9.2 \begin{equation} P(v|θ')=∫P(v|θ,θ') P(θ|θ')dθ \end{equation} Context: Given parameterised distribution, $P(v|θ')$ want to learn optimal parameters $θ$ given some data specify a parameterised prior $P(θ|θ')$ with hyperparameter $θ'$ since we may have some idea of $θ$ that is appropriate. Learning is then finding optimal $θ'$ that maximises likelihood $$P(v|θ')=∫P(v|θ,θ') P(θ|θ')dθ $$
