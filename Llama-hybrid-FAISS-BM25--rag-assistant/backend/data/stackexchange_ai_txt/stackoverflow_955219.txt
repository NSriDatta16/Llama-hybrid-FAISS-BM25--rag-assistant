The session cookie is held in volatile storage accessible to that instance of the browser. You would need a browser that supported the concept you are after, no such browser exists to my knowledge. In ASP.NET you can maintain a session without using a session cookie, its real ugly and I wouldn't recommend it but it may be a way for you acheive your goal.
