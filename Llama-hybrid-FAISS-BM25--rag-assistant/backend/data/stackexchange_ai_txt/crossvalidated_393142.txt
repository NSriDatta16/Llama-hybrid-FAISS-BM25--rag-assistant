Finding the posterior distribution of mean and variance given data sample using Gibbs Sampling?

I have the following hierachical bayesian model - $\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{c},\sigma^2 \sim \mathcal{N}(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{c},\sigma^2)$ $\mathbf{c}|\mathbf{c}_1,\sigma^2_2 \sim \mathcal{N}(\mathbf{c}|\mathbf{c}_1,\sigma^2_2)$ $\sigma^2 \sim \mathcal{U}(\sigma^2|r_1,r_2)$ . Here, $\mathbf{x}$ is a data sample. $r_1$ , $r_2$ , $c_1$ and $\sigma_2^2$ are fixed known values. $\mathcal{U}(\cdot)$ and $\mathcal{N}(\cdot)$ are uniform and normal distributions respectively. The goal is to generate samples from the posterior distribution $p(\mathbf{c},\sigma^2|\mathbf{x})$ .
