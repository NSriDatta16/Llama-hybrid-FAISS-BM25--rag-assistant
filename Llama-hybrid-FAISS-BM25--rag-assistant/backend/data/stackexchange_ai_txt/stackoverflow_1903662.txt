Another trick (which you may not be able to use) is to leverage the automatic build and revision numbers generated by .NET. If your AssemblyInfo has: [assembly: AssemblyVersion("1.0.*")] The last two numbers are just a date/time stamp. Some code (list below) may help: Version v = Assembly.GetExecutingAssembly().GetName().Version; DateTime compileDate = new DateTime((v.Build - 1) * TimeSpan.TicksPerDay + v.Revision * TimeSpan.TicksPerSecond * 2).AddYears(1999); Edit: here's an alternative answer that may be a little clearer to follow than what I put: https://stackoverflow.com/a/804895/2258
