Here's a way to leverage the array printing functions. I probably wouldn't use it, but it comes pretty close to meeting your requirements! a = np.random.rand(5,4) x = np.array('col1 col2 col3 col4'.split()) y = np.array('row1 row2 row3 row4 row5'.split()) b = numpy.zeros((6,5),object) b[1:,1:]=a b[0,1:]=x b[1:,0]=y b[0,0]='' printer = np.vectorize(lambda x:'{0:5}'.format(x,)) print printer(b).astype(object) [[ col1 col2 col3 col4] [row1 0.95 0.71 0.03 0.56] [row2 0.56 0.46 0.35 0.90] [row3 0.24 0.08 0.29 0.40] [row4 0.90 0.44 0.69 0.48] [row5 0.27 0.10 0.62 0.04]]
