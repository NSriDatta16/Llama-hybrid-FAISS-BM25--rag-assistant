How to calculate the Bayesian Risk Classifier

I'm not exactly sure how to calculate the Bayesian risk Classifier $L(r^*)$ for $Y\in\{ 0,1 \}$ . For this scenario, assume: $X\in\mathbb{X}=[0,1],Y\in\{ 0,1 \}$ $\pi_y=P(Y=y)=1/2$ for $y\in{0,1}$ Conditional distributions $[X|Y=y]$ characterised by: $f(x|Y=0)=2-2x$ and $f(x|Y=1)=2x$ . I know the classification loss function for a generic classifier $r:\mathbb{X}\longrightarrow\{0,1\}$ is $\ell(x,y,r(x)=[[r(x)\neq y]]$ where $\ell:\mathbb{X}\times\{0,1\}\times\{0,1\}$ . I'm also aware that associated risk is $L(r)=E([[r(X)\neq Y]])$ which is equivalent to $P(r(X)\neq Y)$ and that $L(r)\geq L(r^*)$ . In the binary classification case, $L(r^*)=E(min\{\tau_1(X),1-\tau_1(X)\}=1/2-1/2E(|2\tau_1(X)-1|)$ , but I'm not exactly sure how to go from that to the $Y\in\{ 0,1 \}$ case with the stated PDFs.
