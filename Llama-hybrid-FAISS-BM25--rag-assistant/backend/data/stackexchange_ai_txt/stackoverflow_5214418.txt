This is called a many to many relationship, or how you usually encounter it: M:N. There is no way around the third table, and while it contains redundant information, it follows the design rules of relational databases. Yes, there are tradeoffs, however that's what you get when you push a technology out of its comfortzone. If you look to keep the redundancy to a minimum, a NoSql solution might be worth looking into, however, this might force you lose a lot of the advantages of an RDBMS. You have to weight your alternatives and go with the storage facility suiting you the most.
