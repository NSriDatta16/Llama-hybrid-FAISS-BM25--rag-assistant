Certainly the authors of ASP.NET MVC In Action advise against it, and I agree. It isn't necessary, so why do it? In the early betas a code-behind file was included, but this was removed at RTM (or shortly before). Typically, it simply encourages you to do more non-view work than you should in the view, as it is out of sight / out of mind.
