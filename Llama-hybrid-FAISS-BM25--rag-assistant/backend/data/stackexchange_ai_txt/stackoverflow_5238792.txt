There is a way: it is called virtual memory (vm). All three operating systems listed will use virtual memory (vm), unless there is no hardware support (which may be true in embedded systems). So I will assume that vm support is present. Here is a quote from the architecture notes of the Varnish project: The really short answer is that computers do not have two kinds of storage any more. I would suggest you read the full text here: http://www.varnish-cache.org/trac/wiki/ArchitectNotes It is a good read, and I believe will answer your question.
