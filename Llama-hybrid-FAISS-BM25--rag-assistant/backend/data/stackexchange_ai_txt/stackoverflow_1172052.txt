Streets are a bad analogy to use for software. Streets cannot be easily moved, rerouted, or changed without significant effort. Well-written software is an composition of orthogonal components that are easily reorganized and modified as needed. The reason that agile development works is that both the developers and the software itself are agile. The developers respond quickly to change and the software they write is written in a manner that makes it responsive to change as well.
