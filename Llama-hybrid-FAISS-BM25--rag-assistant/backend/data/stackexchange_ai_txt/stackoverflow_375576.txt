I would say the practical aspects of coding are more important. In particular, source control is vital if you don't use that already. I like bzr as an easy to set up and use system, though GUI support isn't as mature as it could be. I'd then move on to one or both of the classics about the craft of coding, namely The Pragmatic Programmer Code Complete 2 You could also check out the list of recommended books on Stack Overflow .
