Gibbs sampling Bayesian conditional distribution for mean of a Normal distribution

first post here in CV. I'm currently working on a textbook exercise on Gibbs Sampling and got stuck on naming the distribution for one of the conditional distributions. Question Consider a normal model: $$x_i \sim N(\mu,\sigma^2) \text{ for } i=1,...,N$$ Assuming the prior $$p(\mu,\sigma^2)\propto\frac{1}{\sigma^2}$$ Derive & write the name & parameters for the conditional distribution $p(\mu|\sigma^2,x_1,...,x_{N})$ Attempt so far I've calculated the joint pdf as $$p(x_1,...,x_N,\mu,\sigma^2)\propto (\frac{1}{\sigma^2})^{N/2+1}\exp{(-\frac{1}{2\sigma^2}\sum_1^N{(x_i-\mu)^2})}$$ So the conditional pdf is: $$p(\mu|\sigma^2,x_1,...,x_{N}) \propto p(x_1,...,x_N,\mu,\sigma^2) \\ \propto (\frac{1}{\sigma^2})^{N/2+1}\exp{(-\frac{1}{2\sigma^2}\sum_1^N{(x_i-\mu)^2})} \\ \propto \exp{(-\frac{1}{2\sigma^2}\sum_1^N{(x_i-\mu)^2}}) \\ \propto \exp{(-\frac{1}{2\sigma^2}\sum_1^N{(x_i^2-2\mu x_i +\mu^2)}}) \\ \propto \exp{(\frac{1}{\sigma^2}(-\frac{N}{2}\mu^2+\mu\sum_1^N{x_i}))} \\ \propto \frac{\exp{(\mu\sigma^{-2}\sum{x_i}})}{\exp{(\mu^2\sigma^{-2}\frac{N}{2}})} $$ I'm struggling to see what distribution this one is, with respect to $\mu$ . My question What is the name & parameters for a distribution with the pdf of a ratio of 2 exponentials like this: $$\frac{e^{\alpha x}}{e^{\beta x^2}}$$
