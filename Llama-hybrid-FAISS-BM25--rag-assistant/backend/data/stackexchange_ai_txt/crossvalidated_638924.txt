How to manually evaluate a parameter estimate using the first few values of a time series?

I'm working on a problem (I paraphrase), Consider the $\text{AR}(2)$ model $$y_t=\alpha y_{t-1}-(1-\alpha)y_{t-2}+\epsilon_t, \hspace{1em} \epsilon_t\sim N(0,\sigma^2).$$ The conditional least squares likelihood estimate of $\alpha$ satisfies $$\hat{\alpha}=\frac{\sum_{t=3}^ny_ty_{t-1}+\sum_{t=3}^ny_ty_{t-2}+\sum_{t=3}^ny_{t-2}^2+\sum_{t=3}^ny_{t-1}y_{t-2}}{\sum_{t=3}^ny_{t-1}^2+\sum_{t=3}^ny_{t-2}^2+2\sum_{t=3}^ny_{t-1}y_{t-2}}.$$ If $y_1=1,y_2=2,y_3=0,y_4=1$ , calculate the maximum likelihood estimate $\hat{\alpha}$ of $\alpha$ . I don't know how the first few values of $y_t$ allow me to evaluate the whole expression for $\hat{\alpha}$ , which includes $y_t,t\geq5$ . Could someone give me a pointer?
