This method will not match the estimates you get from a regression of $y$ on $\ln x$, but can be a reasonable approximation in some cases if you don't have the data. It comes from the definition of semi-elasticity . Take the estimated effect of $x$ on $y$ from the original regression (.7) and multiply that by the average value of $x$. This will give you a semi-elasticity of sorts.
