If you are already using ANTLR and have your AST ready you might want to take a look at StringTemplate: http://www.antlr.org/wiki/display/ST/StringTemplate+Documentation Also Section 9.6 of The Definitive ANTLR Reference: Building Domain-Specific Languages explains this: http://www.pragprog.com/titles/tpantlr/the-definitive-antlr-reference The free code samples are available at http://media.pragprog.com/titles/tpantlr/code/tpantlr-code.tgz . In the subfolder code\templates\generator\2pass\ you'll find an example converting mathematical expressions to java bytecode.
