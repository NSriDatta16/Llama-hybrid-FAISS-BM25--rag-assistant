There are certainly organizations that rely on UML, including a few that may expect you to answer OO design questions with UML in an interview. Plus, documentation tools like Doxygen generate UML-like diagrams to describe a class hierarchy. Beyond that though, most groups I've worked with in academia or industry don't really use it. If you want an explanation of why, read " Death by UML Fever ".
