About the evaluation of covariance of linear Gaussian model in PRML

Section 8.1.4 of Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning introduces the linear Gaussian model where each node has distribution $$ p(x_i \big|pa_i) = \mathcal{N}\left ( x_i \Bigg| \sum_{j\in pa_i}w_{ij}x_j + b_i, v_i \right ) $$ and let $$ x_i = \sum_{j\in pa_i}w_{ij}x_j + b_i + \sqrt{v_i}\epsilon_i $$ where $$ \epsilon_i \sim \mathcal{N}(0, 1) $$ Then it shows the process of deriving the covariance: $$ \begin{align} \operatorname{cov}[x_i, x_j] &= \mathbb{E}\left[\left(x_i - \mathbb{E}[x_i]\right)\left(x_j - \mathbb{E}[x_j]\right)\right]\\ &=\mathbb{E}\left [ (x_i - \mathbb{E}[x_i])\left \{ \sum_{k\in pa_j}w_{jk}(x_k - \mathbb{E}[x_k]) + \sqrt{v_j}\epsilon_j \right \} \right ]\\ &=\sum_{k\in pa_j}w_{jk}\operatorname{cov}[x_i, x_k] + I_{ij}v_j \end{align} $$ How to obtain the second term $I_{ij}v_j$?
