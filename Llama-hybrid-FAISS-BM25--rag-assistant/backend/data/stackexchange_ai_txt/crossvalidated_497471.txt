When considering the approximation $$\int_a^b f(x)\,\text dx\ \approx\ \frac{b-a}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N f(x_i)=\hat I$$ the lhs is an average of iid random variables, the $(b-a) f(X_i)$ 's, with variance $$\text{var}\, \hat I = \dfrac{1}{N} \text{var}\, \{(b-a) f(X_i)\}=\dfrac{(b-a)^2}{N} \text{var}\, f(X_i)$$ This variance can thus be approximated by a convergent estimator $$\dfrac{(b-a)^2}{N} \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^n \{f(X_i)-\overline{f(X)}\}^2 =\dfrac{(b-a)^2}{N^2} \sum_{i=1}^n f(X_i)^2 - \left[\frac{(b-a)}{N} \sum_{i=1}^n f(X_i)\right]^2$$
