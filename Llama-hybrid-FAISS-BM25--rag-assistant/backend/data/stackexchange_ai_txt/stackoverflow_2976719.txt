No, the Thread-local storage is unrelated to the startup of a new thread. The thread may be needed due to thread-affinity issues with sub-operations of the GetHostByName API, such as the need to use asynchronous callbacks without affecting the calling thread's re-entrancy. Or it may be a lazy initialization feature of WinSock where a daemon thread needed for a subset of WinSock operations is needed, and this was the first API to require the daemon.
