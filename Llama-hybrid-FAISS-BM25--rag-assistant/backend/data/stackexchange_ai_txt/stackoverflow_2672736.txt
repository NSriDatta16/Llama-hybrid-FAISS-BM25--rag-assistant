In most cases you really only need one instance of ErrorProvider on a form. E.g. ErrorProvider errorProvider1 = new ErrorProvider(); or just drag one from the toolbox onto the form. When calling an ErrorProvider, you supply the control and the message,, errorProvider1.SetError (dateTimePicker1, "HEY BAD DATE"); To clear the error... errorProvider1.SetError (dateTimePicker1, ""); So, one ErrorProvider instance is all you really need in most situations.
