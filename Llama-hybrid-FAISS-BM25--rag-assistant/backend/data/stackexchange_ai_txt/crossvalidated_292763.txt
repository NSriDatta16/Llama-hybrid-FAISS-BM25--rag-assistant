(I'm assuming if they don't upgrade, you make $\$0$.) This is really quite straightforward. If every customer you give your sales pitch to has a $p$ probability of upgrading, and each upgrade will net you, on average, an extra $\$10$, then you expect to make, on average: \begin{align} d &= 10p\qquad \text{(per person)} \\ d &= 10Np\quad \,\text{(total)} \end{align} where $d$ is dollars, and $N$ is the number of customers you give your sales pitch.
