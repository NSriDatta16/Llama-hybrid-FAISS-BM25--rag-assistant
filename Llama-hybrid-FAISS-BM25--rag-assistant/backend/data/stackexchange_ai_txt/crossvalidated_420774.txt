You are right in that it should be interpreted as a percentage. In other words, accuracy changing from 0.08 to 0.06 should be interpreted as a change in accuracy by an average of 2% (average taken across the trees that make up the forest). Here is a link, together with an accompanying paper that you might consult: https://bigdata.unl.edu/documents/ASA_Workshop_Materials/Why%20and%20how%20to%20use%20random%20forest%20variable%20importance%20measures.pdf https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC1796903/
