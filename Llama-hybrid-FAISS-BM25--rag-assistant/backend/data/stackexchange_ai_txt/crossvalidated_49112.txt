I see no problem with your resolution: if the number of states is m, there are $m\times(m-1)$ free parameters when $k=1$ and $m\times m\times(m-1)$ free parameters when $k=2$ . Unless your data is strongly dependent upon the two past states, the likelihood ratio test will favour $k=1$ . If you want to reduce the number of parameters for $k=2$ , you have to do it "by hand", i.e. by introducing restrictions on those $m\times m\times(m-1)$ free parameters... Or use a variable length Markov chain .
