No, $X_t$ and $\varepsilon_t$ are dependent. $X_t$ is a linear function of $\varepsilon_t$ , and $X_t$ is positively correlated with $\varepsilon_t$ : \begin{aligned} \text{Cov}(X_t,\varepsilon_t) &= \text{Cov}([c + \sum_{i=1}^p \varphi_i X_{t-i} + \sum_{i=1}^q \theta_i \varepsilon_{t-i}] + \varepsilon_t,\varepsilon_t) \\ &= \text{Cov}(\sum_{i=1}^p \varphi_i X_{t-i} + \sum_{i=1}^q \theta_i \varepsilon_{t-i},\varepsilon_t) + \text{Cov}(\varepsilon_t,\varepsilon_t) \\ &= 0 + \sigma^2 \\ &> 0. \end{aligned} $X_t$ and $\varepsilon_{t-j}$ are also dependent. Rewrite ARMA as MA( $\infty$ ), and you will find that $X_t$ is a linear function of $\varepsilon_{t-j}$ , and thus an analogous argument to the one above holds also here. No, because of 1.
