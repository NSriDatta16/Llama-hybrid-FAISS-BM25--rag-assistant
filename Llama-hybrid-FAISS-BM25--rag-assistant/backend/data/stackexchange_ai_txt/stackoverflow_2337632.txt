It depends on how the trigger was created. CREATE/ALTER TRIGGER statements support the NOT FOR REPLICATION clause: NOT FOR REPLICATION Indicates that the trigger should not be executed when a replication agent modifies the table that is involved in the trigger. See Controlling Constraints, Identities, and Triggers with NOT FOR REPLICATION .
