Is i(G) equivalent to i_l(G), the local independency set of BN?

Reference: Koller, D., & Friedman, N. (2010). Probabilistic graphical models: Principles and techniques (Nachdr.). MIT Press. Bayesian network $\mathcal{G}$ encodes that for any node $X_i$ , there are always local independencies with any node that isn't a descendant of $X_i$ , with the condition of all parent nodes: $$ \mathcal{I}_{\ell}(\mathcal{G}) := \{X_{i} \perp NonDecendants_{X_i} \mid Pa_{X_i} , \forall i\} $$ However, Koller & Friedman(2010) defined another $I(G)$ , which means all conditional independencies in BN $\mathcal{G}$ . So I have 3 questions: whether $\mathcal{I}_{\ell}(\mathcal{G})$ is equivalent to $I(\mathcal{G})$ ? It seems that a BN only contains local dependencies. the proposition " $P$ factorized over $\mathcal{G}$ " is equivalent to (a) " $\mathcal{I}_{\ell}(\mathcal{G})\subseteq \mathcal{I}(P)$ ", or (b) " $\mathcal{I}(\mathcal{G})\subseteq \mathcal{I}(P)$ "? if we know that $\mathcal{I}_{\ell}(\mathcal{G})\subseteq \mathcal{I}(P)$ , whether $\mathcal{G}$ is an I-map of $P$ ?
