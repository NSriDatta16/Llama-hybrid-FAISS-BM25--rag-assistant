Estimation of parameter $\widehat\beta$ in the linear model

Consider the simple linear model $Y=X\beta+\varepsilon$ where $\varepsilon\sim N_n(0,\sigma^2I).$ It known that $\widehat\beta=(X^tX)^{-1}X^tY$ . Also, $$\pi(\beta\mid Y)\propto\Gamma\left[\frac{1}{2}(v+r) \right] v^{-r/2}S^{-r} \left[1+\frac{(\beta-\widehat\beta) X^tX(\beta-\widehat\beta)} {vS^2}\right]^{-(v+r)/2}$$ which is the kernel of student's t distribution. $\beta$ is a r.v. with student's t distribution. mean $=$ mode $=$ median $=\widehat\beta$ The last beta (mean $=$ mode $=$ median $=\widehat \beta$ ), Is the same as $\widehat\beta=(X^tX)^{-1}X^tY$ or is it other? Supposing it is the same, then I cannot see the sense of calculating the prior,marginal of beta, etc. because this $\widehat\beta=(X^tX)^{-1}X^tY$ can be known without bayesian calculations. What is the point doing the bayesian calculations then?
