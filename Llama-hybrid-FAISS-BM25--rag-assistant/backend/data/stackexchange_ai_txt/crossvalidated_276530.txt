You are combining both steps. Breaking them out (e.g. see here ), you have E step $Q(\theta\mid\theta_\text{old})=\sum_Z p(Z\mid X,\theta_\text{old})\log p(X,Z|\theta)$ M step $\theta_\text{new}=\max_\theta Q(\theta\mid\theta_\text{old})$ For the "E step", you are computing the average $\mathbb{E}\big[\log p(X,Z\mid\theta)\big]$, taking $Z\sim p(Z\mid X,\theta_\text{old})$.
