Start with a simple logistic regression: $\,\,\text{logit}(\mu) \,= \beta_0 + \beta_1 x\quad\quad$ (original) $\quad\quad\quad\quad= \beta_0^* + \beta_1^* (x-\bar{x})/s_x\quad\quad$ (standardized x) $\quad\quad\quad\quad= (\beta_0^* -\beta_1^*\bar{x}/s_x)+ (\beta_1^*/s_x) x$ So $\beta_1=\beta_1^*/s_x$ and $\beta_0=\beta_0^* -\beta_1^*\bar{x}/s_x$ More generally, the same manipulation yields $\beta_0=\beta_0^* -\sum_{i=1}^p\beta_i^*\bar{x_i}/s_{x_i}$ and $\beta_i=\beta_i^*/s_{x_i}$
