Let $\{X_n:n=0,1,2\ldots\}$ be a Markov chain with transition matrix $P$ . Then the $(i,j)$ -entry of $P$ is the probability of transitioning from state $i$ to state $j$ in $n$ steps: $$ P_{ij} = \mathbb P(X_n = j\mid X_0 = i). $$ Here we have $$ P = \left( \begin{array}{cc} \frac{9}{10} & \frac{1}{10} \\ \frac{1}{2} & \frac{1}{2} \\ \end{array} \right),\quad P^2 = \left( \begin{array}{cc} \frac{43}{50} & \frac{7}{50} \\ \frac{7}{10} & \frac{3}{10} \\ \end{array} \right), $$ and in general $$ P^n = \left( \begin{array}{cc} \frac{1}{6} \left(\left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^n+5\right) & \frac{1}{6} \left(1-\left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^n\right) \\ \frac{5}{6} \left(1-\left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^n\right) & \frac{1}{3} \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n-1}+\frac{1}{6} \\ \end{array} \right), $$ so that $$ \lim_{n\to\infty} P^n = \begin{pmatrix} \frac56&\frac16\\\frac56&\frac16. \end{pmatrix} $$ Hence $X_n$ has limiting distribution $$ \lim_{n\to\infty} \mathbb P(X_n = j) = \begin{cases}\frac56,&j=\mathrm{sunny}\\ \frac16,&j=\mathrm{rainy} \end{cases}, $$ independent of the distribution of $X_0$ .
