Add a shell wrapper around your command and capture the pid. For my example I use iostat. #!/bin/sh echo $$ > /tmp/my.pid exec iostat 1 Exec replaces the shell with the new process preserving the pid. test.sh | grep avg While that runs: $ cat my.pid 22754 $ ps -ef | grep iostat userid 22754 4058 0 12:33 pts/12 00:00:00 iostat 1 So you can: sleep 10 kill `cat my.pid` Is that more elegant?
