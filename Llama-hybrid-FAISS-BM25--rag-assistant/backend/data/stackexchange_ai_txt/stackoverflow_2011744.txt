3-tier architecture can have different meanings depending on context. Generally it means that responsibilities in the application are divided between different tiers. Typically, 3-tier refers to : presentation tier" (actual user interface) logic tier (application/business logic) data tier (database, data storage) The details vary by application. Wikipedia, as usual, has a nice overview: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multitier_architecture A simple example would be a typical business app: presentation: browser, or fat client logic tier: business logic, typically in an application server (based on J2EE, ASP.NET or whatever) data tier: a database, typically a RDBMS such as MySQL or Oracle
