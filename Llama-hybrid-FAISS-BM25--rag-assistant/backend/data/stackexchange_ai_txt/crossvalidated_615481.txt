The marginal density of a probability density is again a probability density (a.s.). The same notations should not be used for different entities, so let me define $$\pi_i(x_i) = \int_{\mathbb R^{n-1}} \pi(x_1, \ldots, x_n) dx_1 \ldots dx_{i-1} dx_{i+1} \ldots dx_n$$ as the $i$ -th marginal. Then $$\int_{\mathbb{R}} \pi_i(x_i) dx_i = \int_{\mathbb{R}}\int_{\mathbb R^{n-1}} \pi(x_1, \ldots, x_n) dx_1 \ldots dx_{i-1} dx_{i+1} \ldots dx_n dx_i\\ =\int_{\mathbb R^{n}} \pi(x_1, \ldots, x_n) dx_1 \ldots dx_{i-1} dx_i dx_{i+1} \ldots dx_n = 1$$ by Fubini's Theorem . This applies to the Bayesian setting in the question: if the joint posterior is proper, then any posterior marginal or conditional is equally proper (a.s.).
