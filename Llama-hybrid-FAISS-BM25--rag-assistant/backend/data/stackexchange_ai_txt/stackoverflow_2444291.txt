You configure cherrypy to use sessions and store them to a file, e.g. in this way: cherrypy.config.update({'tools.sessions.on': True, 'tools.sessions.storage_type': "file", 'tools.sessions.storage_path': "/tmp/cherrypy_mysessions", 'tools.sessions.timeout': 60}) (or similarly in the config file of course), then cherrypy.session is the "per-user" dict you want, and cherrypy.session['a'] = 1 and similarly for 'b' is how you can store data there.
