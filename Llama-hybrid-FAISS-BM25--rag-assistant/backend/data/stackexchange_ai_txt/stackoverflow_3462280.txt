For small amounts of data, you can do what you propose. For larger amounts of data, you wouldn't be doing a join anyway, even if you were using a SQL database for your backend. Your problem is pretty much the typical social networking data problem. Most likely, you will want to denormalize your data, and "push" information to all of a user's friend list whenever they update their status, or possibly pull it as you are doing above. The best solution depends on the shape of your data and what kinds of queries you want to optimize for - retrieval or updates. See my answer here for more info, including a post by a facebook engineer.
