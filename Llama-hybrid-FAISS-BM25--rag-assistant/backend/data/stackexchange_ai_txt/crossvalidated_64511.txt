You should probably make an assumption of normality for starters and see what the data look like. You've probably heard about analyzing proportion data with logistic regression or other specific proportion data relating to underlying binomial or multinomial systems. Your data aren't like that . Proportions like yours can be pretty much normally distributed but could take on other distributions as well. You're going to have to examine your actual data and come back here once you get an idea of the kinds of distributions of results you have.
