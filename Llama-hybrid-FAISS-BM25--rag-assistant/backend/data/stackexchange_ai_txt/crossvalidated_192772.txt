Convergence criteria for Linear Process time series models

For the model $X_t = \sum_{j=-\infty} ^{\infty} \psi_j Z_{t-j}$, where $Z_t \sim WN(0, \sigma^2)$, I'm not totally clear on why we require $\sum_{j=1}^{\infty} | \psi_j| I think we can show that $$ E \left[\sum_{j=-\infty} ^{\infty} |\psi_j Z_{t-j}| \right] \le \sigma \sum_{j=-\infty} ^{\infty} |\psi_j|$$ so the left hand side converges if the right hand side does. But why is it important for the left hand side to converge?
