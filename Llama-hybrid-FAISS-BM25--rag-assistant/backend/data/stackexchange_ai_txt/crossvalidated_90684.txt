The first thing I would do is make some graphs of BMI over time per person and color them as to whether they were pre-onset or post-onset. Then I'd think about whether the trend in BMI pre-onset is an interesting predictor. If it is, there are some methods that allow time varying covariates that are not regular; or, you could try some sort of cluster analysis (either formal or not) on the trends and then see if those were related to onset. Survival analysis - time to event - may be more powerful than methods that simply look at onset vs. not (logistic regression). It may also be a more interesting question, substantively
