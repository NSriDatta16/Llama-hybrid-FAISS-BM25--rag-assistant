numeric is a decimal (base-10) fixed-point datatype; float is a binary (base-2) floating-point datatype. A numeric[18,10] defines a decimal with precision (maximum total number of decimal digits that can be stored, both to the left and to the right of the decimal point) 18 and scale (maximum number of decimal digits that can be stored to the right of the decimal point) 10. It consumes 9 bytes of storage to a float's default 8 bytes. Here's a starting point for more reading .
