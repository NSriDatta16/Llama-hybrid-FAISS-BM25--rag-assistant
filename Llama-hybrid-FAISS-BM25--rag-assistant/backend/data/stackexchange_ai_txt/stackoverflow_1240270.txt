At least for ISO C++, this is well-defined (excepting long long for obvious reasons) by the Standard in 3.9.1[basic.fundamental]/2: There are four signed integer types: “signed char”, “short int”, “int”, and “long int.” In this list, each type provides at least as much storage as those preceding it in the list. Note that this is speaking of storage, not value ranges. This specifically means sizeof .
