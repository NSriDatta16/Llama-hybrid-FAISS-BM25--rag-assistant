Bounding the distance of empirical average from its expected value

Suppose we have three sequences of random variables, $(Y_n)_n$ , $(W_n)_n$ , and $(X_n)_n$ such that: If $Y_n=a$ , then $X_n=b$ . If $X_n=b$ , then $W_n=c$ . That is $$ 1_{[Y_n=a]}\leq 1_{[X_n=b]}\leq 1_{[W_n=c]} $$ for all $n=1,2,\dots$ , where $a,b,c$ are real numbers. $(1_{[Y_n=a]})_n$ are i.i.d.; $(1_{[W_n=c]})_n$ are i.i.d. $\Pr(1_{[Y_n=a]}=1)=\nu_{a}$ and $\Pr(1_{[W_n=c]}=1)=\nu_{c}$ for all $n=1,2,\dots$ , where $\nu_a \leq \nu_c$ . Some implications of the above: A. $\Pr\Big(\Big|\frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^n 1_{[Y_k=a]} -\nu_{a}\Big|\geq \epsilon \sqrt{\frac{\nu_{a}(1-\nu_{a})}{n}\Big)}\le \frac{1}{\epsilon^2}$ $\forall \epsilon>0$ $ \forall n$ (Chebyshev inequality) B. $\Pr\Big(\Big|\frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^n 1_{[W_k=c]} -\nu_{c}\Big|\geq \epsilon \sqrt{\frac{\nu_{c}(1-\nu_{c})}{n}\Big)}\le \frac{1}{\epsilon^2}$ $\forall \epsilon>0$ $ \forall n$ (Chebyshev inequality) C. $\Pr\bigg(\liminf_{n\rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^n 1_{[X_k=b]}\geq \nu_a\bigg)=1$ D. $\Pr\bigg(\limsup_{n\rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^n 1_{[X_k=b]}\leq \nu_c\bigg)=1$ Can we use the above relations to bound the distance between $\frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^n 1_{[X_k=b]}$ and its mean?
