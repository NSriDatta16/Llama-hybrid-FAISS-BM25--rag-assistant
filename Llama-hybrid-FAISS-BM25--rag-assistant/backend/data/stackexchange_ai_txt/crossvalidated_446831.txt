Split plot with RBCD in R: Are inferences valid when output reports some effects are not estimable?

Data are from an agricultural experiment and are the proportion of turnips damaged by cabbage maggot in different insecticide treatments (note preliminary investigation suggested data were approximately normal and homoskedastic). There were three whole-plot treatments applied at planting time ("atplant") arranged in 5 randomized complete blocks ("block"). Each whole plot was split into three subplots assigned one of three postplant treatments ("postplant"). "Wholeplot" indicates the wholeplot number. data.frame(block,wholeplot,atplant,postplant,propdam) fit.aov = aov(propdam~atplant*postplant + block + Error(wholeplot), data=vz19) summary(fit.aov) fit.aov The ANOVA results seem reasonable, but the Given this, I suspect the error is not critical but before publishing results I want to make sure inferences are sound.
