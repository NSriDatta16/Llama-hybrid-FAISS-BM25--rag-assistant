No, it is not possible in a portable manner across all browsers. You can't tell the browser to render a piece of text differently without inherently changing the DOM, regardless of whether you do it statically or dynamically (with Javascript, for example, as a post processing step). Edit: In Chrome starting around 2019, you can use Scroll To Text Fragment , but this is not an HTML or browser standard feature.
