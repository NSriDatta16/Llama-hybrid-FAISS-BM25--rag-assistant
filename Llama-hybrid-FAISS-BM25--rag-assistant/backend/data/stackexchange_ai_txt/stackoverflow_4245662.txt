In your example ... you only need to release the ivar in -(void)dealloc; My practice is to only access ivars through the Accessor/Mutator (getters/setters), so when I allocate and initialize an ivar I do the following. NSMutableData *lXMLData = [[NSMutableData alloc] init]; self.xmlData = lXMLData; [lXMLData release]; I find it keeps everything nicely organised and balanced I have also seen self.xmlData = [[[NSMutableData alloc] init] autorelease] ; (but I'm not a fan) My approach ... Access ivars only through Accessors/Mutators Alloc/Init a local var Assign local var to ivar (class variable) Release local var
