Cookies will still be around as an option just like Session is still an option for server-side. In terms of Html5 client side storage options, it's one of the more volatile parts of the specification still. Many/most browsers are support Html5 to some degree; however, how they are implementing the storage isn't consistent yet since it's changed in the spec a couple times. To paraphrase Diodeus's answer... Cookies aren't broke so no need to fix or replace them. Html5 storage is just another option.
