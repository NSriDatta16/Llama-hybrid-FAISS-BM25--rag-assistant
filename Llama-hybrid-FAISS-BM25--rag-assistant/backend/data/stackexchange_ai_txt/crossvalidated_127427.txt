No. Suppose you have an interaction between two dummy variables with 5 and 4 levels respectively. Then there are 20 levels in the interaction. Under the null hypothesis (the features are totally unrelated to the target), you would expect to see on average one out of these 20 features reach significance at the 0.05 level (because that's what a p-value is). Under those circumstances it would be a mistake for the analyst to interpret the lone significant interaction.
