By default no headers designed to control caching are sent. This leaves the client to make up its own rules about how to cache the content sent. So yes you would need something like:- Response.Cache.SetCacheability(HttpCacheability.NoCache) to ensure that a request (redirect or otherwise) does not simply use a cached version.
