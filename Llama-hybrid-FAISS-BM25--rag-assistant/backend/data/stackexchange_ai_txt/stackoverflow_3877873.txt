Do not shrink the database during maintenance. There is probably no other more damaging action you can do. Read more at Auto-shrink â€“ turn it OFF . IF a database has grown to a certain size, then it will likely grow back if you shrink it. Shrinking the database is tremendously damaging to the index fragmentation and will slow down your reporting and analytic workloads. Once shrink, when the database will grow back during normal operations the auto-growth events will interrupt and freeze the database during the growth. There is one thing to shrink a database that had got out of control due to some rogue action that increased it. But to have the shrink in maintenance task means you will constantly do it on a scheduled interval, and this is very bad .
