The most elegant way is to have in your CSS something like: .exampleclass img {height:250px} .js .exampleclass img {height:auto;} and in your JS: var dd = document.documentElement; dd.className += ((dd.className == "") ? "js" : " js"); so that the second CSS rule will override the first one in case JS is enabled
