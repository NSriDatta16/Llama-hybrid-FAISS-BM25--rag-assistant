The Proxy Design Pattern will help you achieve this while this is the pattern used for plugins. Another short explanation is with the Gang of Four - Proxy Design Pattern . Having an eye out the Shadow Copying of assemblies is definitely useful for the trick, as you shadow copy your updated assembly to your application assemblies folder, then you can make your application aware of changes, then unload the old assembly and load the new one for the changes to take effect, that is, without even having to restart your application, and this will be absolutely transparent for the end-user. A more simple approach might be the ClickOnce Deployment which you might be interested to read about following the sugested link.
