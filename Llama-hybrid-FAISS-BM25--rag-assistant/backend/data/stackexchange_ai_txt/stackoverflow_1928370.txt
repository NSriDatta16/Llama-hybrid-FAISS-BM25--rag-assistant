I just tested this by writing a subclass of UITextField subclass that captures touches. @interface TouchTestTextField : UITextField {} @end #import "TouchTestTextField.h" @implementation TouchTestTextField -(void) touchesBegan:(NSSet *)touches withEvent:(UIEvent *)event{ NSLog(@"touchesBegan"); NSLog(@"touches=%@,event=%@",touches,event); } @end It easily captures touches and shows the text field as the view of the touch.
