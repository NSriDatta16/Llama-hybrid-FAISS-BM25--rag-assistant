Random walk on $\mathbb{Z}$

Let $X_n=\sum\limits_{k=1}^n Y_k = X_{n-1} + Y_n$ and $X_0=0$, $(Y_n)$ i.i.d with $P(Y_n=1)=p=1-P(Y_n=-1)=1-q, p \in (0,1)$, $(X_n)$ is a random walk on $\mathbb{Z}$. Why is this Markov chain irreducible? How do we know that all states (integers) communicate with each other? How do we show this intuitively?
