Check out the examples in the Boost Regex library. If you edit your question to give a better idea of what exactly you are looking for in your robots.txt file, someone can help you with the Regex syntax. For example, if you are trying to find the names of all User-agents in the file, you could use an expression like this. boost::regex expression("^User-agent:\s*(.*)");
