Because the iid assumption implies $$\text{Cov}(X_i - \overline{X},\overline{X})=\text{Cov}(X_j - \overline{X},\overline{X})$$ for all $i$ and $j$, take the average of $\text{Cov}(X_i - \overline{X},\overline{X})$ (which the bilinearity of $\text{Cov}$ allows you to do by averaging the first argument $X_i - \overline{X}$) to obtain $$\text{Cov}(X_i - \overline{X},\overline{X}) = \text{Cov}(\overline{X} - \overline{X},\overline{X}) = \text{Cov}(0, \overline{X}) = 0.$$
