In this case you don't need to specify applicationContext in your JNLP. The href you have mentioned above is expecting the path of the jar file, not the xml. Bundle the xml as part of the jar and then, somewhere in your startup code, load your spring application context using the (bundled) xml and ClassPathXmlApplicationContext.
