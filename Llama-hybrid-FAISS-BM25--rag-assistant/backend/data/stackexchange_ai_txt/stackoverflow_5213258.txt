You could use persistent cookies . In ASP.NET when you emit a cookie you could set the Expires property to some date into the future. This will create a persistent cookie on the client browser and you will be able to fetch the value back even if the user closes/reopens the browser again. And because we live in 2011 and HTML5 is knocking on our doors (it has even entered inside some homes) it is worth looking into the HTML5 local storage .
