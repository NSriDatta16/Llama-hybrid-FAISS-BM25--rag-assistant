ZFS is a start. Many storage vendors provide 520B drives with extra data protection available as well. However, this only protects your data as soon as it enters the storage fabric. If it was corrupted at the host level, then you are hosed anyway. On the horizon are some promising standards-based solutions to this very problem. End-to-end data protection. Consider T10 DIF (Data Integrity Field). This is an emerging standard (it was drafted 5 years ago) and a new technology, but it has the lofty goal of solving the problem of data corruption.
