I don't recommend k-fold for time series data, since you shouldn't be randomizing the inputs and the time series sequence must be preserved. However, you can do your own permutations of the training data (t1,t2,t3), (T2,t3,t4), (T2,t3,t4,t5), and still preserve your anomalies in the test data set. These answers may also be helpful: https://stats.stackexchange.com/questions/14099/using-k-fold-cross-validation-for-time-series-model-selection
