Here Samy Bengio explains CCA which is what you may try first. It can give you the info how similar are two matrices residing in some spaces with one dimension in common. CCA -- canonical correlation in the experimental context is to take two sets of variables and see what is common among the two sets. So it is general enough I would say. R has the standard function cancor and several other packages, including CCA and vegan.
