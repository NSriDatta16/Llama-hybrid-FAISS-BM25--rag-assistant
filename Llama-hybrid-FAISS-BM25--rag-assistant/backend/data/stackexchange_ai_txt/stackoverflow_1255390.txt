It would be beneficial if the students were at some point exposed to tools that can help them write cleaner, better code. The tools may not all be relevant to them at this stage, but knowing what is available helps. Debuggers - gdb, totalview, ... Static/dynamic analysers - splint , valgrind , ... Unit testing frameworks - CUnit , cmockery , Check , ... Documentation management - doxygen , ... Build management - Make, memoize , fabricate , ... Code metrics - CCCC , Understand , SLOCCount , ... Code coverage - gcov, LCOV, ... One should also stress the use of different (!) compilers with strict compiler warning flags and attending to each and every warning message.
