A common approach is to construct some kind of ARMA model . The easiest way to do so is by windowizing the time series with a certain window length N: stock prices at time $k-N$ to $k-1$ are used to predict the stock price at time $k$. You can, ofcourse, include additional parameters for prediction. As an example, suppose we have the following univariate time series $s$: 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8 9 $=s[1]..s[9]$ Windowizing using $N=3$ yields: $$\begin{align} \Big[s[k-3],\ s[k-2],\ s[k-1]\Big] &\rightarrow s[k] \\ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 2 & 3 & 4 \\ 3 & 4 & 5 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 6 & 7 & 8 \end{bmatrix} &\rightarrow \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 5 \\ 6 \\ \vdots \\ 9 \end{bmatrix} \end{align}$$ In this example, for each discrete moment $k$, we obtain 3-dimensional $\mathbf{x}$ vectors to predict $y=s[k]$. The window length $N$ becomes a tuning parameter which must be optimized, for example using cross-validation.
