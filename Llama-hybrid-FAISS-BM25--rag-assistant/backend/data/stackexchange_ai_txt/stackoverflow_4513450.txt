Not natively with a Range. You can mess with its mind but you're better off using an incremented first value. This is ugly but: [(1..5).to_s].map{ |s| a,b=s.split('..').map{|i| i.to_i}; (1+a .. b) } #=> [2..5] or Range.new(*[(1..5).to_s].map{ |s| a,b=s.split('..').map{|i| i.to_i}; [1+a,b] }.flatten) #=> 2..5 or irb(main):004:0> asdf = (1..5) => 1..5 irb(main):005:0> Range.new(asdf.min.succ, asdf.max) => 2..5 None seem overly elegant.
