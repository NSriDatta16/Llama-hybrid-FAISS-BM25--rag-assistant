On the other hand, you can also do things like this: char (*t)[100] = s; if(t == &t[0]) printf("true 3. "); if(t == &t) printf("true 4."); In that case, t is indeed a pointer to an array of characters that has its own storage and address. Case 3 is true and case 4 is false .
