It's kind of hard to answer this question regarding the proper organization of cases A and B and test methods 1, 2 and 3... However splitting the tests to test cases serves two major purposes: 1) Organizing the tests around some logical groups, such as CustomerViewTests, OrdersAggregationTests, etc. 2) Sharing the same setUp() and tearDown() methods, for tests which require the same, well, setup and tear down. More information and examples can be found at unitTest documentation .
