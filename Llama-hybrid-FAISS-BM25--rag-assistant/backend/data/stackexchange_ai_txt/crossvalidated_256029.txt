I think this problem is rather similar to Example 8.2.8 in my book, The Bayesian Choice . Example 8.2.8 (Example 8.2.4 continued) For the chi-squared distribution,the natural parameterization is $$ \theta={1\over \sigma^2}, \quad T(y)=-{1\over 2}y, \quad \psi(\theta)=-{p\over 2}\log(\theta), $$ and $$ \int_0^c e^{-\gamma_0\lambda\theta} \theta^{-\lambda p/2} \,d\theta $$ is infinite if $\lambda p \ge 2$. Similarly, $$ \int^{+\infty}_c e^{-\gamma_0\lambda\theta} \theta^{-\lambda p/2} \,d\theta = +\infty $$ if $\gamma_0\lambda 0,\ 0\le a\le {1\over p+2}. $$
