I'm not familiar with JAGS but your problem is quite straight forward. The reason we do MCMC is to estimate the posterior distribution of a particular parameter(s). As a result, once you finished running the MCMC, you have a full posterior distribution of your parameters. You can use R's quantile function and the posterior distribution as the input to estimate the parameter values at the $.025$ and $.0975$ quantiles. Then just use those parameter values to plot using something similar to cf[1] + cf[2]*x1 + cf[3]*x2 code.
