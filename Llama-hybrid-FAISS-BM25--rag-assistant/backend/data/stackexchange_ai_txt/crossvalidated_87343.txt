In theory: No ; each side of a perfect n -sided die has a 1 in n chance of appearing. In the real world: Yes ; die shapes are imperfect, often favoring opposing sides, hence the tradition of making sure opposing sides are equal on average. As explained in this answer . Having all high numbers on one side also makes it more easy to fake fair rolls, like merely spinning or skittering the die.
