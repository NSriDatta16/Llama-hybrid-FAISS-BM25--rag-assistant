You have a few approaches. The simplest is not to declare the variable with my . But this requires you to avoid use strict; and not recommended as a result. You could declare your variable outside the functions at the top of your script. This variable would then be available to all functions below. This is a consequence of scope : variables declares outside a set of curly braces are generally available inside any subsequent curly braces. You could declare your variable using the use vars qw/$myvar/; pragma. This inherently makes your variable available throughout the following code.
