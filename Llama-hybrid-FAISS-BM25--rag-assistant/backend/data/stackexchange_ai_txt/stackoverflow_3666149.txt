Your problem is known as the arrowhead anti-pattern. There are pragmatic approaches, such as the Guard statements you've shown in your sample, to whole design patterns, avoiding if (and else) all together... Lots of resources on how to solve them: http://www.codinghorror.com/blog/2006/01/flattening-arrow-code.html http://www.lostechies.com/blogs/chrismissal/archive/2009/05/27/anti-patterns-and-worst-practices-the-arrowhead-anti-pattern.aspx http://elegantcode.com/2009/08/14/observations-on-the-if-statement/
