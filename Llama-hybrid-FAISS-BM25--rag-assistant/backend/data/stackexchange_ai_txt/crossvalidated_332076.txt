Sampling from Bayesian Hierarchical Logistic Regression Posterior

Suppose we fit a Bayesian logistic regression model of the form $$Y_i \sim Bernoulli(p_i)$$ $$logit(p_i) = \beta_0 + \beta_1*x + \alpha_{j[i]}$$ $$\alpha_j \sim N(0,\sigma_\alpha^2)$$ $$\beta_i \sim N(0,1000)$$ and we obtain samples from MCMC of $$(\beta_0^{(s)},\beta_1^{(s)},\alpha_j^{(s)})$$. Suppose we wish to calculate the 95% Credible Interval for cluster 1. Is it equivalent to take the quantile of $$logit^{-1}(\beta_0^{(s)} +\beta_1^{(s)}*x + \alpha_1^{(s)}) , \ \forall s$$ or take $$logit^{-1}(\beta_0^{.025/.975} + beta_1^{.025/975}x + \alpha_1^{.025/.975})$$ That is, can we take the quantiles of the coefficients first?
