It's part of their background image: https://www.purevolume.com/_images_new/header_nav_bg.png This is, of course, one of many ways to accomplish this. In Chrome and Firefox you can use CSS: -webkit-border-radius:5px; -moz-border-radius:5px; Of course this won't work for Internet Explorer. For IE you would have to use a different technique - there are many. Doing the rounded corners in your background image is safe, but locks you into a box to where you have to edit images in order to change the visual shape of an element (not that bad, but not always encouraged).
