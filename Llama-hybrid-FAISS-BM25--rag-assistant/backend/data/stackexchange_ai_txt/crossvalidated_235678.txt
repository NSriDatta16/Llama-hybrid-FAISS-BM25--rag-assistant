Why is $x + x = 2x$, but $X + X \neq 2X$?

At this AP central page Random Variables vs. Algebraic Variables , the author, Peter Flanagan-Hyde draws a distinction between algebraic and random variables. In part he says $x + x = 2x$, but $X + X \neq 2X$ -- in fact it's the subtitle of the article. What is the basic difference between an algebraic variable and a Random Variable?
