Normally, when counting the number of parameters in a neural network, you are referring to the total size of the matrices, not the number of matrices. With the given data, we have that: $U \in \mathbb{R^{4\times3}}$ → 12 parameters $W \in \mathbb{R^{3\times3}}$ → 9 parameters $b \in \mathbb{R^3}$ → 3 parameters $V \in \mathbb{R^{3\times2}}$ → 6 parameters $c \in \mathbb{R^2}$ → 2 parameters Totalling 32 parameters ( = 9 + 12 + 3 + 6 + 2).
