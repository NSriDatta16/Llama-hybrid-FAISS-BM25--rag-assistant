No, as also pointed out in the comments, it's just the same name used for different concepts. In machine learning, kernel commonly describes an inner product representing an underlying feature transformation. In linear algebra, it's just the null space, i.e. $\ker(A)=$ $\{x \ |\ Ax=0\}$ .
