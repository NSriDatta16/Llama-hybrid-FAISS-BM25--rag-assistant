This is sometimes called the hierarchical ordering principle : “Hierarchical ordering […] is a term denoting the observation that main effects tend to be larger on average than two-factor interactions, two-factor interactions tend to be larger on average than three-factor interactions, and so on” ( Li, Sudarsanam, & Frey, 2006, p. 34 ). It is discussed most often (as far as I can tell) in the literature on Design of Experiments. In particular, it is one of the main ideas motivating and justifying the use of fractional factorial designs . My first ever blog post was about exploring some different possible reasons why hierarchical ordering might be true more often than not: Cookie Scientist: The hierarchical ordering principle
