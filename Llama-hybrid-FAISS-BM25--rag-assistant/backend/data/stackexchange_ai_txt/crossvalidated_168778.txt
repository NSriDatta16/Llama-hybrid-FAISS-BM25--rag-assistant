I think it depends on who you ask. According to this article There are various opinions on the importance of this assumption with regard to the parameters interpretation. Some authors state that violation from it is nothing extremely problematic as in such cases parameter for a covariate for which assumption is not satisfied can be understood as ‘average effect’ over timepoints that are observed in a dataset (Allison, 1995). The others however underline the importance of this assumption (Hosmer, Lemeshow 1999) and suggest potential modification of the model if hazard ratio turns out not to be constant over time for some covariates.
