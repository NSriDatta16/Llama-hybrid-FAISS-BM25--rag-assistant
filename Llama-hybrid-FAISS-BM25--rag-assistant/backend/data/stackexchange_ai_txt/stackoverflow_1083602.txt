Check the Twitter API changelog. They are blocking requests from EC2 that don't have a User-Agent header in the HTTP request because people are using EC2 to find terms to spam. Twitter recommends setting the User-Agent to your domain name, so they can check out sites that are causing problems and get in touch with you.
