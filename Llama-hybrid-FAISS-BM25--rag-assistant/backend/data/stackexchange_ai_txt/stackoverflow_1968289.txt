The greatest of all human faculties is the power of abstraction. That is what separates us from the animals. The more we exercise this power, the more successful we are in solving problems. Playing chess may seem a futile pastime to some and never of practical use to any, but it goes a long way to give the player the ability to think ahead every time an important decision is to be made. Besides, it reveals the elegance and simplicity that is hidden beneath layers of ugly syntax and brain-dead code we sift through every day just to make a living.
