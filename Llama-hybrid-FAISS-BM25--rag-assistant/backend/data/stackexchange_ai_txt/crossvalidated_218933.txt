I've read that the non-parametric bootstrap can be seen as a special case of a Bayesian model with a discrete (very)non informative prior, where the assumptions being made in the model is that the data is discrete, and the domain of your target distribution is completely observed in your sample. Here are two references: The bootstrap and Markov chain Monte Carlo, Bradley Efron The Non-parametric Bootstrap as a Bayesian Model, Rasmus Bååth
