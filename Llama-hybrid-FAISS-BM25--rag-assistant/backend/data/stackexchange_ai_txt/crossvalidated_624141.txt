Deriving autocovariance of time series

Consider the following AR(1) process: $$ y_{t}=\phi_{1}y_{t-1}+\epsilon_{t} $$ I am asked to compute the autocovariance betweem $y_{t}$ and $y_{t-s}$ . Write down the DGP for $y_{t-s}$ which is: $$ y_{t-s}=\phi_{1}y_{t-s-1}+\epsilon_{t-s} $$ Write down the covariance as: \begin{alignat*}{1} cov\left(y_{t},y_{t-s}\right) & =E\left(\left(\phi_{1}y_{t-1}+\epsilon_{t}\right)\left(\phi_{1}y_{t-s-1}+\epsilon_{t-s}\right)\right)\\ & =E\left(\phi_{1}^{2}y_{t-1}y_{t-s-1}+\epsilon_{t}\epsilon_{t-s}+\phi_{1}\epsilon_{t-s}y_{t-1}+\phi_{1}y_{t-s-1}\epsilon_{t}\right)\\ & =\phi_{1}^{2}E\left(y_{t-1}y_{t-s-1}\right) \end{alignat*} Where the last expression follows from the white-noise properties of $\epsilon.$ I know that the correct answer is in fact $\sigma_{y}^{2}\phi_{1}^{s},$ but I am unable to get to it. Any tips are much appreciated.
