$AR(\infty)$ representation $y_t(1-0.6B+0.08B^2)=\epsilon_t(1-0.5B)$

Let the process $\{y_t\}$ $$y_t=0.6y_{t-1}-0.08y_{t-2}+\epsilon_t-0.5\epsilon_{t-1}$$ where $\epsilon_t$ is White Noise with zero mean and variance $\sigma^2$. Is possible to find the $AR(\infty)$ representation? First I write the process using lag operators $$y_t(1-0.6B+0.08B^2)=\epsilon_t(1-0.5B)$$ If a process have $AR(\infty)$ representation the roots of Moving Average polynomial are $>|1|$. $$1-0.5B=0\Rightarrow B=2$$ so the process is invertible. To find the representation I followed some notes that I found $$\epsilon_t=\frac{(1-0.6B+0.08B^2)}{1-0.5B}=[(1-0.6B+0.08B^2)\sum_{j=0}^\infty (0.5B)^j]x_t$$ $$\epsilon_t=[\sum_{j=0}^\infty 0.5^j B^j-0.6\sum_{j=0}^\infty 0.5^j B^{j+1}+0.08\sum_{j=0}^\infty 0.5^j B^{j+2}]x_t$$ $$\epsilon_t=[1+\sum_{j=0}^\infty (0.5B)^{j+1}-0.6\sum_{j=0}^\infty 0.5^j B^{j+1}+0.08\sum_{j=0}^\infty 0.5^j B^{j+2}]x_t$$ $$\epsilon_t=[1+0.5B-0.6(0.5)B+\sum_{j=0}^\infty 0.5^j B^{j+2}(0.5^2-(0.6)(0.5)+0.08)]x_t$$ $$\epsilon_t=[1+0.5B-0.6(0.5)B-0.02\sum_{j=0}^\infty 0.5^j B^{j+2}]x_t$$ $$\epsilon_t=x_t+0.2x_{t-1}+0.03\sum_{j=2}^\infty 0.5^{j-2}x_{t-j}$$ Is it right?
