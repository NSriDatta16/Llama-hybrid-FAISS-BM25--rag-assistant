Floating points can't represent some integers exactly, so there is no method that is truly reliable . Specifically, any int that is greater than 2^24 cannot be represented exactly by a float . Alas, that is part of the price you pay for using a floating-point representation! For an excellent summary of the various issues with floating point that you should be aware of, I encourage you to check out "What Every Computer Scientist Should Know About Floating-Point Arithmetic" .
