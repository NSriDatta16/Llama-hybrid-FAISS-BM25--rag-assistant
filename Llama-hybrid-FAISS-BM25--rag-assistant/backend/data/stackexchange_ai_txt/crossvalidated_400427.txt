Adding to what users said, if you average many uncorrelated or little correlated variable you get a reduction in variance. Define $X=\sum_{i}^{B}T_i$ where $T_i$ is a single decision tree, B number of decision tree. $Var(X)=\frac{1}{B^2}\sum_i\sum_jcov(X_i,X_j)=\frac{1}{B^2} \sum_{i} ( \sum_{i\neq j} Cov(X_i,X_j)+Var(X_i)) = \frac{1}{B^2}\sum_ i ( (B-1)\sigma^2\rho +\sigma^3)=\frac{B (B-1)\sigma^2\rho+B\sigma^2}{B^2}=\rho\sigma^2+\sigma^2\frac{1-\rho}{B}$ . First quantity decreases if $\rho$ decreases to zero ( uncorrelation) and second quantity decreases if B increases.
