It turns out that averaging the predictions of many simple regressions is closely related to Partial Least Squares -- specifically, with suitable scaling of variable it gives exactly the first component of a univariate-response PLS model, and the successive components come from doing the same thing to residuals from the earlier components. The comparison is described in "An Interpretation of Partial Least Squares" by Paul Garthwaite
