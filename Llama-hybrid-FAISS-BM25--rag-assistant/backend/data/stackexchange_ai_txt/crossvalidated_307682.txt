You need all the covariances between the coefficients, so you cannot do this with the data given. The elasticity in even a simple univariate model like $E[y \vert x] = \alpha + \beta x$ is $$\frac{\partial E[y \vert x]}{\partial x} \cdot\frac{x}{E[y \vert x]}=\beta \cdot \frac{x}{\alpha+\beta x}=\frac{\beta}{\frac{\alpha}{x}+\beta}.$$ This is a ratio of two functions of random variables, $\alpha$ and $\beta$. Call the denominator $\eta$. Using a Taylor series expansion, you can show that $$Var \left( \frac{\beta}{\eta} \right) \approx \frac{Var(\beta)}{\eta^2}-\frac{2 \cdot \beta}{\eta^3}\cdot Cov(\beta,\eta)+\frac{\beta^2}{\eta^4}\cdot Var(\eta)$$ You need the covariance between $\alpha$ and $\beta$ to calculate the last two terms. Your proposal to treat $\frac{x}{\hat y}$ as constant amounts to ignoring those two terms, which will usually understate your variance.
