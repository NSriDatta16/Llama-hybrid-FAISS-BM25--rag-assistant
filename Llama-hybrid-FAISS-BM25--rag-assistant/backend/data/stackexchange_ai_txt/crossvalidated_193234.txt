Yes, the problem is that auto.arima expects a ts time series - or an object that can be coerced to that. Internally, auto.arima coerces x . You can check what this looks like by: as.ts(tt) ## Time Series: ## Start = 16563 ## End = 16654 ## Frequency = 1 ## [1] 1494.50 NA 1367.57 NA NA NA NA 1357.57 NA ## [10] 1222.23 NA NA NA NA 1124.02 NA 1011.64 NA ## [19] NA NA NA 4575.64 3201.87 3050.04 2173.38 1967.88 1838.55 ## [28] 1666.05 NA 1656.05 1524.96 NA NA NA NA NA ## [37] 835.96 NA NA NA NA NA NA 775.36 592.36 ## [46] 494.15 NA NA NA 4058.15 2624.36 2448.47 NA NA ## [55] 1598.47 1398.47 1264.14 1165.88 1053.67 NA NA NA NA ## [64] NA NA 941.36 NA NA NA NA 821.36 NA ## [73] 471.36 NA NA 373.15 NA NA NA 259.91 NA ## [82] NA NA NA 3808.91 2262.26 1940.39 1011.39 800.81 NA ## [91] NA 790.81 Thus, zoo detects the underlying numeric time index corresponding to the Date s (number of days since 1970-01-01), creates a regular grid, and fills it with NA s. What to do in this situation depends on whether this is your complete data and what the pattern is that you expect to be relevant. For example, one could try to coarsen the data to a regular grid or try to fit a continuous time model etc.
