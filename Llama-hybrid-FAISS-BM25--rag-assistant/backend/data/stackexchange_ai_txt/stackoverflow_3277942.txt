Instead of a just use a element to preserve formatting, you can .load() into it the exact same way. The default behavior of HTML is to display without line-breaks, extra white-space, etc (except in a ), from the HTML4 spec : For all HTML elements except PRE , sequences of white space separate "words" (we use the term "word" here to mean "sequences of non-white space characters"). When formatting text, user agents should identify these words and lay them out according to the conventions of the particular written language (script) and target medium.
