What does Box and Unbox mean?

Possible Duplicates: Why do we need boxing and unboxing in C#? What is boxing and unboxing and what are the trade offs? In C# what does "Box and Unbox" mean? Here's an extract from MSDN where I found the text. But this convenience comes at a cost. Any reference or value type that is added to an ArrayList is implicitly upcast to Object. If the items are value types, they must be boxed when they are added to the list, and unboxed when they are retrieved. Both the casting and the boxing and unboxing operations decrease performance; the effect of boxing and unboxing can be very significant in scenarios where you must iterate over large collections.
