Stability of VAR$(p)$: Prove $\det(I_{Kp}-\tilde{A}z)=\det(I_k-A_1z-\ldots-A_pz^p)$

Given we have a VAR$(p)$ process written in the companion form $$\tilde{y}=\tilde{v}+\tilde{A}\tilde{y}_{t-1}+\tilde{u}_t$$ where $$\tilde{A}=\left(\begin{array}{ccccc} A_1& A_2 & \ldots & A_{p-1} & A_p \\ I_k& 0 & \ldots & 0 & 0 \\ 0& I_k & & 0 &0 \\ \vdots& &\ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 0& 0&\ldots & I_k & 0 \end{array} \right).$$ (I follow the notation from LÃ¼tkepohl, New Introduction to Multivariate Time Series ) Exercise 2.1 (p. 67) asks to show $\det(I_{Kp}-\tilde{A}z)=\det(I_k-A_1z-\ldots-A_pz^p)$ in order to use the common test for stability of the process. However, it seems like I do not have sufficient knowledge in theory regarding determinants of block matrices. I really do not know how to solve $$\det\left(\begin{array}{ccccc} I_k-A_1z& -A_2z & \ldots & -A_{p-1}z & -A_pz \\ -I_kz& I_k & \ldots & 0 & 0 \\ 0& -I_kz & & 0 &0 \\ \vdots& &\ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ 0& 0&\ldots & -I_kz & I_k \end{array} \right).$$ I have a couple of properties for matrices of the form $\left(\begin{array}{cc}A&B\\C&D\end{array} \right)$ but I do not see how this helps me to get to the solution. Any hints are welcome.
