The following is based on section 9.2 of Hamilton's Time Series, with suitable adaptations. This complements the answer by @AlecosPapadopoulos I'll try to prove that $E(\hat{\mathbf{z}}_i\mathbf{z}'_i)$ is full column rank (the new instruments are correlated with the regressors. To generate the new instruments, we first need to use OLS on the following equation: $$ z_{il}=\mathbf{x}'_i\beta_l+\epsilon_{il} $$, where $z_{il}$ is element $i,l$ of matrix $\mathbf{Z}$. By OLS, we know that $\hat{\beta}_l=(\sum^N_{i=1}\mathbf{x}_i\mathbf{x}'_i)^{-1}(\sum^N_{i=1}\mathbf{x}_iz_{il})$. So, $\hat{\mathbf{z}}'_i=\mathbf{x}'_i\hat{\beta}$, where $\hat{\beta}=(\hat{\mathbf{\beta}}_1 \dots \hat{\mathbf{\beta}}_L)= (\sum \mathbf{x}_i\mathbf{x}'_i )^{-1}(\sum \mathbf{x}_i\mathbf{z}'_i)$ From $1/N \sum_i\hat{\mathbf{z}}_i\mathbf{z}'_i=\hat{\beta}'\frac{\sum \mathbf{x}_i\mathbf{z}'_i}{N}=(\sum \mathbf{z}_i\mathbf{x}'_i)(\sum \mathbf{x}_i\mathbf{x}'_i )^{-1}(\sum \mathbf{x}_i\mathbf{z}'_i)/N$ If we do the plim on both sides, we get $E(\hat{\mathbf{z}}_i\mathbf{z}'_i)=E(\mathbf{z}_i\mathbf{x}'_i)(E(\mathbf{x}_i\mathbf{x}'_i))^{-1}E(\mathbf{x}_i\mathbf{z}'_i)$ Now in the IV setting $K=L$ (numb.regressors=numb.instruments), so $E(\mathbf{x}_i\mathbf{z}'_i)$ is a square matrix and by assumption it's full column rank (the x's are also instruments). The $E(\mathbf{x}_i\mathbf{x}'_i)$ is invertible, since we're also assuming conditional homoskedasticity, and together with the assumption of $E(\mathbf{g}_i\mathbf{g}'_i)$ being non-singular , where $\mathbf{g}_i=\mathbf{x}_i\epsilon_i$ is a martingale sequence.Therefore, $E(\hat{\mathbf{z}}_i\mathbf{z}'_i)$ is invertible.
