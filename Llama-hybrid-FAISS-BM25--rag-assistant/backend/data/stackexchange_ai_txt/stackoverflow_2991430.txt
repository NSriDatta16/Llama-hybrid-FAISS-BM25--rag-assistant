The size of storage required and how big the numbers can be. On SQL Server: tinyint 1 byte, 0 to 255 smallint 2 bytes, -2 15 (-32,768) to 2 15 -1 (32,767) int 4 bytes, -2 31 (-2,147,483,648) to 2 31 -1 (2,147,483,647) bigint 8 bytes, -2 63 (-9,223,372,036,854,775,808) to 2 63 -1 (9,223,372,036,854,775,807) You can store the number 1 in all 4, but a bigint will use 8 bytes, while a tinyint will use 1 byte.
