Not at all sure, why you want to change id on clone at the same time while dragging. But, why don't you apply the changes to the clone, after you drop it over. I mean the clone helper any way, will do your thing while dragging. And you make a clone when you drop, and apply other changes (like, changing the id, etc) on that clone. Something like this.. $(document).ready(function(){ $(".objectDrag").draggable({helper:'clone'}); $("#garbageCollector").droppable({ accept: ".objectDrag", drop: function(event,ui){ var clone = $(ui.draggable).clone(); $(clone).attr('id', 'new_id') $(this).append(clone); } }); });
