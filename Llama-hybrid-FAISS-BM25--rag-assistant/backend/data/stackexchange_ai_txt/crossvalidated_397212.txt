If you insist on using this as your dependent variable, estimate a censored regression, since the dependent variable is bound on the interval [0,1]. As mentioned in the comment above, logistic regression would be interesting, if you have investor-specific (beyond gender). A dummy variable for gender would allow you to look at an individual's probability of investing (though you may not have adequate data for this. It's hard to tell from your description. Other interesting questions could come from looking at the size of investments, rather than measuring investments as a binary variable. By looking at the proportion of investors, you aren't distinguishing between someone investing one dollar and someone else investing one million dollars.
