There are some gotchas to be taken into account with this approach Depending on your session storage system, your session may grow too big if the objects you are putting in it are complex. The default store for sessions in Rails 2.3 are cookies so there will definitely be a limit imposed by the browser. If your model changes, after deploying your app users with old sessions will be presented with objects marshalled from the old model definitions, so they may get unexpected errors.
