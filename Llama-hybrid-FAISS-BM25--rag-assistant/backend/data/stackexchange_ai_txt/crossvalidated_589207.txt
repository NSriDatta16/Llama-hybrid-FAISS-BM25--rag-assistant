If $Var(X)=\sigma_X^2$ and $Var(Y)=\sigma_Y^2$ and $Cor(X,Y)=\rho$ , and assuming without loss of generality $E[X]=E[Y]=0$ , so $E[X^2]=E[Y^2]=\sigma^2$ , then $Cov(X,Y)=E[XY]=\rho \sigma_X^{\,} \sigma_Y^{\,}$ , and thus $Var(X-Y)= E[(X-Y)^2]=E[X^2]-2E[XY]+E[Y^2]= \sigma_X^2 - 2\rho \sigma_X^{\,} \sigma_Y^{\,} + \sigma_Y^2$ . If $\sigma_X^2=\sigma_Y^2 = \sigma^2$ we can simplify this to $Var(X-Y)=2(1-\rho)\sigma^2$ . In this question, that is the variance of the change in blood pressure of an individual. The division by $n$ is to see the variance in the average change across $n$ individuals.
