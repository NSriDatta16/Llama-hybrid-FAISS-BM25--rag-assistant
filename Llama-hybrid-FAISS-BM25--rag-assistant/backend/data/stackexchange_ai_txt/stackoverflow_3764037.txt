Download jline from here and copy the jar to the directory from where the clojure.jar is loaded. The clj script should find and enable it. On the other hand if you use leiningen to organize your projects, which I heartily recommned, then you do not have to do anything other than run lein repl and the REPL starts with the expected history behavior and all dependencies of your project on the classpath.
