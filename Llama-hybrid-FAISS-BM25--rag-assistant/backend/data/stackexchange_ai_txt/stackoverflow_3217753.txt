A JFrame , like all top-level containers , makes an excellent view implementation; I typically use JPanel to organize the JComponent subclasses that constitute related view elements. The model should have no knowledge of the view(s); it notifies observers (listeners) of changes in state, typically in response to the invocation of its methods. An application may have more than one model, and each model may have more than one view. Swing itself uses a related separable model architecture , as discussed in A Swing Architecture Overview .
