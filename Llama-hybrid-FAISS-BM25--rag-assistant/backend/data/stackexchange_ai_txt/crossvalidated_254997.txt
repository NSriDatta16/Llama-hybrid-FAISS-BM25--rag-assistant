You can do two pairwise chi-squared tests (outcome vs exposure 1, outcome vs exposure 2), or you can fit a logistic regression in the form of: $$logit(outcome)=exposure_1 + exposure_2$$ This can be easily implemented in a statistical software like R. Here is something that might help you with fitting a logistic regression in R: https://cran.r-project.org/web/packages/HSAUR/vignettes/Ch_logistic_regression_glm.pdf .
