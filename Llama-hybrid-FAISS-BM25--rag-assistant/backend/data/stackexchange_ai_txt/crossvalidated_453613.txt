calculate R² as a function of μ,σ

Suppose I fit the sum of $t$ iid random variables $$y_t=\sum_{i=1}^t{x_i} \space \space \space\space\space x_i \text{ i.i.d} \sim N[\mu,\sigma]$$ to a linear regression model $$f_t=a\space t +b$$ then I calculate the coefficient of determination $R^2$ $$R^2=RSQ([y_1..y_n],\space [f_1..f_n])$$ I expect that on average $R^2$ will depend on $\mu$ and $\sigma$ : $$E[R^2]=f(μ,σ)$$ does such a relationship exist? What is the function $f()$ ?
