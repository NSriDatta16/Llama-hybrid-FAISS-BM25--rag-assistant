Cloud computing is about hardware-based services (involving computing, network and storage capacities), where: Services are provided on-demand ; customers can pay for them as they go, without the need to invest into a datacenter. Hardware management is abstracted from the customers. Infrastructure capacities are elastic and can easily scale up and down. There is a powerful economic force behind this simple model: providing and consuming cloud computing services generally allows to have far more efficient resource utilization , compared to self-hosting and data center type of hosting.
