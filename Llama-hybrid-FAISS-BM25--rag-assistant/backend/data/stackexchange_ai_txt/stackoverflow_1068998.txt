Database storage with proper indexes will most likely always be faster for random access but might take alot of effort to split records into their individual data elements. Maybe meet in the middle and store the entire record in a single datafield keyed based on whatever unique identifier you would be using to query the data. If you just want to try and 'trim' down the xml files and have control over the schema - something as simple as making the node names a single character or two character has saved me alot of bandwidth/filesize in the past, of course the tradeoff is the readability of the xml goes away. b
