Most (if not all) existing NER data work on the sentence level, so models trained on those datasets also expect its input to be a sentence. Sentence-splitting is part of most NLP toolkits and works remarkably well. In most cases, rule-based procedures such as in NLTK . More advanced tools use machine-learned tokenization, such as UDPipe (best used via spacy-udpipe wrapper) or Stanza both working for many languages.
