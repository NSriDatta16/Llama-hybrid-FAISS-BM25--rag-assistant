If you are using TDD, then you stop unit testing when all the tests in the test list have succeeded. Otherwise, you stop unit testing when the cost of finding more bugs through unit testing exceeds the cost of finding them through your QA process. and when you've reached an acceptable level of code coverage through the combination of all tests.
