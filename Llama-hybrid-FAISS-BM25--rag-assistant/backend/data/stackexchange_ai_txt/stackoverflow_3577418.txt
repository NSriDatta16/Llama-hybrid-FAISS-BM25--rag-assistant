I ran into a similar problem. Here's a solution that is even simpler (if less elegant). I found that this: dataGridView.DataSource = null; dataTable.BeginLoadData(); foreach(DataRow row in dataTable.Rows){ //modify row } dataTable.EndLoadData(); dataGridView.DataSource = dataTable; ...is way faster than this: dataTable.BeginLoadData(); foreach(DataRow row in dataTable.Rows){ //modify row } dataTable.EndLoadData(); Cheers--DC
