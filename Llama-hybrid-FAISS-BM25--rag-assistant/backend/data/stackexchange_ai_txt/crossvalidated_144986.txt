This flow chart is limited, but boils things down well for people new to the field . Beyond that, the dominating factors tend to be What's available in my libraries? What am I comfortable with? Which models have assumptions that match my data? That last one is quite a deep question, and the ability to answer it effectively only comes with a lot of study. If you've got a good grounding in linear algebra and multivariable calculus, a general ML book like Machine Learning: A Probabilistic Perspective would be a good place to start.
