$p(\mu | D, \alpha) \propto p(D | \mu) p(\mu | \alpha)$ bayesian inference

$p(\mu | D, \alpha) \propto p(D | \mu) p(\mu | \alpha)$ bayesian inference. Why does this proportion hold? From bishop's PRML P77. D is the data from a distribution. $\mu$ is the parameters of this distribution. $\alpha$ is the parameters of the prior distribution of $\mu$ .
