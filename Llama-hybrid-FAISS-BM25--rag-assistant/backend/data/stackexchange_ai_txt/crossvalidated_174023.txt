Kalman filter, Local level model

My question is regarding some calculations in the lecture notes “ State-Space Models and Kalman Filters ” by Ruey Tsay. In the local level model we have (p. 1): $$y_{t}=\mu_{t}+e_{t},\; e_{t}\sim N\left(0,\:\sigma_{e}^{2}\right) $$ and $$\mu_{t+1}=\mu_{t}+\eta_{t},\; \eta_{t}\sim N\left(0,\:\sigma_{\eta}^{2}\right) $$. Let (p. 3) $$F_{t-1}=\left\{ y_{1},\, y_{2},\, y_{3},\,\ldots,\, y_{t}\right\} $$ $$y_{t\mid t-1}=E\left(y_{t}\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)=E\left(\mu_{t}+e_{t}\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)=E\left(\mu_{t}\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)=\mu_{t\mid t-1} $$ and $$v_{t}=y_{t}-y_{t\mid t-1}=y_{t}-\mu_{t\mid t-1} $$. Further, $$Var\left(\mu_{t}\left|F_{j}\right.\right)=\sum_{t\mid j} $$ Then Tsay finds on p. 4: $$Cov\left(\mu_{t},\, v_{t}\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right) \\=E\left(\mu_{t}v_{t}\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)\\ =E\left(\mu_{t}\left(y_{t}-\mu_{t\mid t-1}\right)\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)\\ =E\left(\mu_{t}\left(\mu_{t}+e_{t}-\mu_{t\mid t-1}\right)\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)\\ =E\left(\mu_{t}\left(\mu_{t}-\mu_{t\mid t-1}\right)\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)+E\left(\mu_{t}e_{t}\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)\\ =E\left(\left(\mu_{t}-\mu_{t\mid t-1}\right)^{2}\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)\\ =Var\left(u_{t}\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)\\ =\sum_{t\mid t-1} $$ and notes: Where we have used the fact that $$E\left(\mu_{t\mid t-1}\left(\mu_{t}-\mu_{t\mid t-1}\right)\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)=0$$ (Eq. 2). I am a bit confused over what is going on from the $4^{th} $ equality sign to the $5^{th} $ equality sign. When I multiply the parenthesis' out I get: $$E\left(\mu_{t}^{2}\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)=\sum_{t\mid t-1} $$. To complicate things further, Tsay shows in his book, Analysis of Financial Time Series , p. 495 (2nd ed.) the following: $$Cov\left(\mu_{t},\, v_{t}\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)\\ =E\left(\mu_{t}v_{t}\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)\\ =E\left(\mu_{t}\left(y_{t}-\mu_{t\mid t-1}\right)\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)\\ =E\left(\mu_{t}\left(\mu_{t}+e_{t}-\mu_{t\mid t-1}\right)\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)\\ =E\left(\mu_{t}\left(\mu_{t}-\mu_{t\mid t-1}\right)\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)+E\left(\mu_{t}e_{t}\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)\\ =E\left(\left(\mu_{t}-\mu_{t\mid t-1}\right)^{2}\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)\\ =Var\left(\mu_{t}\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)\\ =\sum_{t\mid t-1} $$ In his book he does not use $u_{t} $ but $\mu_{t} $ in the $2^{nd} $ last equality sign. Again he notes that he has used: $$E\left(\mu_{t\mid t-1}\left(\mu_{t}-\mu_{t\mid t-1}\right)\left|F_{t-1}\right.\right)=0$$ (Eq. 2). My question is: What am I missing here and what is going on from the $4^{th} $ equality sign to the $5^{th} $ equality sign?
