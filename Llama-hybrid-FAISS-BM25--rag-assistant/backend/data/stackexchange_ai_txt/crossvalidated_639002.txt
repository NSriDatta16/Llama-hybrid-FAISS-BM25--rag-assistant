Error propagation. Simpler average errors

I am designing a lab practicum to study error propagation. Let's suppose I will measure $x \pm\varepsilon_x$ and $y \pm \varepsilon_y$ , where $\varepsilon_x = \varepsilon_y = \varepsilon$ for simplicity. Both variables are related to the mathematical expression: $z=c \frac{x}{y}$ , where $c$ is a constant. I want to determine the error $\delta z$ to obtain $z \pm \delta z$ , so: $\delta z = |\frac{\partial z}{\partial x}|\varepsilon+|\frac{\partial z}{\partial y}|\varepsilon = c\varepsilon ({ \frac{1}{y}+\frac{x}{y^2}})$ . Measuring three times $x$ and $y$ , I will end up with three values: $z_1 \pm \delta z_1$ , $z_2 \pm \delta z_2$ , and $z_3 \pm \delta z_3$ . I want to obtain a mean value. Is it correct to do the following calculation? $\bar{z} \pm \delta z = \frac{z_1 + z_2 + z_3}{3}\pm \frac{\delta z_1 +\delta z_2 +\delta z_3}{3}$ Thanks in advance.
