James Joseph FitzPatrick, III (born February 1, 1964) is a former American football offensive tackle.

James Fitzpatrick was born in Heidelberg, Germany in 1964. He began his Football career in Beaverton, Oregon where he was a two way starter for the State Champion Beaverton High School Beavers. He attended USC (University Of Southern California) where he was an outstanding offensive tackle. He played in the 1985 Rose Bowl against Ohio State defeating them 20â€“17. During his senior year, he was voted Offensive MVP (Most Valuable Player), extremely unusual for an offensive lineman.

He was selected in the first round (13th overall) by the San Diego Chargers in 1986. He played five seasons for the Chargers. He was traded to the then Los Angeles Raiders in 1990 where he returned to the Colosseum for the remainder of his career.

James now lives in Portland, Oregon, he is active in youth football.

College career
He played college football at the University of Southern California, where he was voted Offensive MVP his senior year.

Professional career
FitzPatrick played in the National Football League from 1986 to 1989 for the San Diego Chargers and 1990 to 1991 for the Los Angeles Raiders.
