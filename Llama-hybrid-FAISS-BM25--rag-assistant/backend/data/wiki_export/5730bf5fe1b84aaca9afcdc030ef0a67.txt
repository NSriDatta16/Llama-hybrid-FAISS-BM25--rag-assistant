Setepenre is an often-used title of Egyptian kings (pharaohs), meaning "Elect of Re". It was also used as a personal name in at least two instances.

Pronunciation
In Akkadian records, the name (referring to Ramesses II) is rendered in cuneiform script as ลกรก-te-ep-na-ri/e-a. According to the Egyptologist Antonio Loprieno, the word was likely pronounced  ().
