The modifier letter right half ring () is a character of the Unicode Spacing Modifier Letters range, used to transliterate:

It is used in romanization to transliterate the Semitic letter aleph after it was used by The Encyclopedia of Islam—later the International Journal of Middle East Studies— , representing the sound /ʔ/ (a glottal stop, Arabic ء hamza). In informal contexts, the backtick ⟨⟩ or the apostrophe ⟨⟩ is commonly used as a substitute.
