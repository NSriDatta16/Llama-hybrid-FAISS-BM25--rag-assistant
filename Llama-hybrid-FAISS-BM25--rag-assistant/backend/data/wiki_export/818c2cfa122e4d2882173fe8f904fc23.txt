Auguste Boyer (13 March 1896 â€“ 21 October 1956) was a French professional golfer prominent on the European circuit in the 1920s and 1930s. He won four Italian Opens, four German Opens, three Swiss Opens, two Belgian Opens, and one Dutch Open. He also finished runner-up three times in his home French Open.

Boyer was born in Cagnes-sur-Mer and died in Nice.
