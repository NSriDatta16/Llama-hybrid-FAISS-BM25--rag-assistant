The Breakthrough of the Year is an annual award for the most significant development in scientific research made by the AAAS journal Science, an academic journal covering all branches of science. Originating in 1989 as the Molecule of the Year,  and inspired by Time Person of the Year, it was renamed the Breakthrough of the Year in 1996.

Molecule of the Year 

 1989 PCR and DNA polymerase
 1990 the manufacture of synthetic diamonds
 1991 buckminsterfullerene
 1992 nitric oxide
 1993 p53
 1994 DNA repair enzyme
