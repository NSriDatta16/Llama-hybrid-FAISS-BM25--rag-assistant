The Kohala Bridge is a bridge across the Jhelum River, a tributary of the Indus River, that forms part of one of the land routes from the Azad Kashmir to Punjab in Pakistan. It is located on the E75 expressway.

The bridge is located in the town of Kohala,  north of Murree and 35Â km south of Muzaffarabad. A bridge was constructed in 1877 and vanished in an 1890 flood. A new transportable steel bridge was constructed in 1899, and in 1990 it too vanished in a flood.  A third bridge was constructed on the north edge of Union Council Birote Kalan, Abbottabad District, in 1993.
