Brommafjorden is a lake in the municipality of Nes in Buskerud county, Norway. Brommafjorden is part of the Hallingdal River (Hallingdalselva).  This is possibly the widest and most quiet part of the Hallingdal River. Brommafjorden was the site of Bromma station,  a now-defunct rail station on the Bergen Railway. It was opened in 1907 when Bergen Railway was opened to Gulsvik and was operated until 1984.
