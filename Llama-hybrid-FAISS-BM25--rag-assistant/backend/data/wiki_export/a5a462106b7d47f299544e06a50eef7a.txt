Thebaud Brothers was an American commission house located in New York City. The company was established around 1792 by Joseph Thebaud, who hailed from France and served as an agent for the French East India Company. From 1862 until its eventual collapse in 1907, Thebaud Brothers held a prominent position as a major importer of Henequen fiber from Mexico into the United States.
