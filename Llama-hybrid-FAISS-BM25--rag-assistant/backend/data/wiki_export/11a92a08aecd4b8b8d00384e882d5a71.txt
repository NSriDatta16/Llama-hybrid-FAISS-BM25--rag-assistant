In the Shahnameh
Ferdowsi's great 11th century epic poem, the Shahnameh, begins with the story of Keyumars.  He was the first king to arise among humans, who at that time lived in mountain caves and wore the skins of leopards. Keyumars was also the first human to introduce royal practices and the preparation of food and was also the first practitioner of law and justice. He was so powerful that all humans, tame animals, and wild animals paid homage to him.
